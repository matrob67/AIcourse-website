## Mistral Enters the Reasoning Game

OpenAI's o1 launched the reasoning model race. DeepSeek-R1 democratized it in open-source. Now Mistral, Europe's AI champion, enters the arena with **Magistral**.

And true to its transparency philosophy, Mistral offers something that neither OpenAI nor DeepSeek provide as clearly: a **fully visible** chain of thought. You don't just receive the answer — you see exactly how the model arrived at it.

<Callout type="info" title="Magistral: transparent by design">
Unlike o1 (whose thinking remains hidden), Magistral **shows you its complete reasoning**. The reasoning tokens are visible in the response. You can read the step-by-step thinking process, identify exactly where an error crept in, and judge for yourself whether the reasoning is sound.
</Callout>

## Architecture and Training

### The Four-Step Pipeline

1. **Pre-training**: Magistral starts from Mistral Large, already a very capable model on its own
2. **SFT on reasoning**: supervised training on high-quality chains of thought, to learn the structure of reasoning
3. **Reinforcement learning**: RL optimization so that reasoning is not only structured but also **correct**
4. **Alignment**: RLHF (Reinforcement Learning from Human Feedback) and DPO (Direct Preference Optimization) so that responses match human preferences (clarity, usefulness, safety)

### The Structured Chain of Thought

What distinguishes Magistral is the readability of its reasoning. The format is structured and explicit:

```xml
<thinking>
The problem asks to find...

Step 1: Identify the variables
  x = unknown
  3x + 15 = 42

Step 2: Set up the equation
  3x + 15 = 42

Step 3: Solve
  3x = 42 - 15 = 27
  x = 27 / 3 = 9

Verification: 3(9) + 15 = 27 + 15 = 42 ✓
</thinking>

The answer is x = 9.
```

It's clear, structured, and most importantly **verifiable**. You can follow each step and immediately spot if something is off. It's the difference between a student who shows you their scratch work and one who just gives you the final answer.



## The Variants: Choose Your Power

### Magistral Small

The entry-level model, optimized for **speed and efficiency**:
- Modest size, deployable locally on common hardware
- Low latency, suitable for interactive applications
- Strong on everyday STEM problems
- Excellent cost/benefit ratio for prototyping and common tasks

### Magistral Medium

The balance between performance and accessibility:
- Noticeably deeper reasoning than Small
- Handles more complex problems reliably
- Good latency via the Mistral API
- The default choice for everyday production

### Magistral Large

The flagship, designed for the most demanding challenges:
- Best performance across all benchmarks
- Long and detailed reasoning chains on problems with 50+ steps
- Comparable to market leaders (o1, DeepSeek-R1) on major benchmarks
- More expensive, but justified for truly difficult problems

<Callout type="tip" title="Guide: choosing your Magistral">
**Small** = prototyping, everyday STEM tasks, local deployment.
**Medium** = everyday production, best cost/quality ratio.
**Large** = the hardest problems, when every performance point counts.
</Callout>

## Technical Corner: Why Transparency Changes Everything

### Concrete Advantages of Visible Reasoning

Unlike hidden-reasoning models (like o1), Magistral's transparency opens up practical possibilities:

- **Debugging**: when the answer is wrong, you see exactly which step the reasoning went off track. No need to guess
- **Trust**: you can read the reasoning and decide for yourself whether the logic holds up, instead of blindly trusting
- **Education**: in pedagogical contexts, showing the solving process is as important as giving the answer
- **Regulatory audit**: for applications in regulated sectors (finance, healthcare), reasoning traceability isn't a bonus — it's a requirement

### Granular Control via the API

The Mistral API allows fine-grained configuration of Magistral's behavior:
- Limit the number of thinking tokens to control costs
- Force or disable reasoning mode per request
- Access thinking tokens separately in the API response

This level of control is valuable for production integration.
