## A Pivotal Moment: Thinking Before Speaking

Since the beginning of LLMs, the process was always the same: you ask a question, the model immediately starts generating its answer, token by token, with no thinking time. It's like asking someone to solve a complex integral by answering instantly, without scratch paper.

In September 2024, OpenAI breaks this paradigm with o1. **o1 doesn't answer right away. It thinks first.**

Concretely, the model generates a long **invisible** chain of thought — hundreds, sometimes thousands of internal tokens where it decomposes the problem, explores leads, checks its calculations, backtracks when it goes wrong, then synthesizes its final answer. It's like watching a mathematician scribble on scratch paper before giving you their clean, clear answer.

<Callout type="info" title="Hidden tokens: paying for thinking">
When o1 receives a question, it first generates **reasoning tokens** — internal thinking that you never see in the final answer, but that you still pay for through the API. Then it produces the visible answer. The thinking stays private, only the result is shared. It's a radical change in how an LLM works.
</Callout>

## How o1 Was Trained

### Beyond Classic RLHF

o1 starts from standard pre-training, then adds something fundamentally new: **reinforcement learning (RL) specifically designed for reasoning**. Classic RLHF (Reinforcement Learning from Human Feedback) aligns the model with human preferences, but doesn't teach it to reason. o1 goes further.

The key idea: instead of rewarding only the final answer (right or wrong), you also reward the **quality of the thinking process**. A structured, methodical reasoning chain that verifies its steps is rewarded, even if it arrives at the wrong answer. A lucky guess without solid reasoning is rewarded less.

### What the Model Learns to Do

- **Decompose** complex problems into manageable sub-problems
- **Explore** multiple approaches when the first leads nowhere
- **Verify its own calculations** internally, like a student proofreading their exam
- **Backtrack** when it detects it has gone down a dead end
- **Synthesize** everything into a clean, structured final answer

### The Revolutionary Scaling: More Time = Better Answer

This is the conceptual key of o1: **inference-time scaling**. Before o1, getting better answers required a bigger model (more parameters). That was the only known scaling dimension.

o1 opens an entirely new dimension:

- **Before**: bigger model = better answers
- **Now**: same model + more thinking time = better answers

It's like the difference between hiring a more qualified expert (parameter scaling) and giving your current expert more thinking time (inference scaling). Both approaches work, and they combine.

## The Results: o1 Crushes Benchmarks

| Benchmark | GPT-4o | o1-preview | o1 |
|---|---|---|---|
| AIME 2024 (math competition) | 13.4% | 56.7% | 83.3% |
| Codeforces (competitive programming) | 808 | 1673 | 1891 |
| GPQA Diamond (PhD-level science) | 53.6% | 73.3% | 78.0% |
| MATH (math problems) | 76.6% | 94.8% | 96.4% |

The numbers speak for themselves. **83% on AIME competitive math**, where GPT-4o caps at 13%. This is the kind of difference that can't be explained by a "slightly better" model — it's a qualitatively different capability.

<Callout type="paper" title="Expert-level performance">
o1 rivals human expert mathematicians on the GPQA benchmark (PhD-level questions) and ranks among the top competitors worldwide on AIME and Codeforces. This is no longer "AI that helps" — it's AI performing at a professional level on complex reasoning problems.
</Callout>

## Where o1 Excels

- **Mathematics**: multi-step problems, formal proofs, math competitions
- **Code**: complex algorithms, non-trivial debugging, competitive programming
- **Science**: advanced physics, chemistry, doctoral-level biology
- **Logic**: puzzles, constraint satisfaction, planning

## Where o1 Is Overkill (and Counterproductive)

- **Simple factual questions**: "Who won the 2022 World Cup?" — no need to think for 30 seconds
- **Creative writing**: o1 "thinks too much" and produces rigid rather than fluid text
- **Text summarization**: a complex chain of thought for a simple task is a waste
- **Anything that doesn't require multi-step reasoning**: the overhead in latency and cost isn't justified

## Variants: Choose Your Level

### o1-mini
Smaller, faster, excelling specifically in STEM (science, tech, engineering, math). Cheaper with better latency. The optimal choice when your problem is purely technical.

### o1-pro
Even more thinking time allocated, thus even better performance on the hardest problems. Available for ChatGPT Pro subscribers.

## Technical Corner: The Cost-Quality Trade-off

o1 is **significantly more expensive** than GPT-4o, because reasoning tokens are billed at the same rate as output tokens:

| Model | Input cost | Output cost | Reasoning tokens |
|---|---|---|---|
| GPT-4o | $2.50/M | $10/M | None |
| o1-mini | $3/M | $12/M | ~10x the output |
| o1 | $15/M | $60/M | ~10x the output |

But the real point isn't the price: it's that **o1 solves problems that GPT-4o simply cannot solve**, regardless of the number of attempts. The trade-off isn't "more expensive for better" — it's "possible vs impossible."

<Callout type="tip" title="2026 context: o1 is retired, reasoning is in GPT-5">
Since GPT-5 (2025), OpenAI has unified all its models: GPT-4o, o1, o3, o4-mini have been merged into the GPT-5 family. Chain-of-thought reasoning is now natively integrated in GPT-5.4 (March 2026), without the need to choose a separate reasoning model. The legacy of o1 remains fundamental: it proved the concept.
</Callout>

<Callout type="warning" title="The reasoning black box">
o1's reasoning tokens are **invisible** to the user. You cannot audit the thinking process that led to the conclusion. This is problematic for critical applications (medical diagnosis, legal decisions) where reasoning transparency is as important as the result.
</Callout>

## The Impact on the Field

o1 inaugurated the era of **reasoning models**. The fundamental message: you don't need ever-larger models or ever-more data for better answers — you just need the model to **think longer**.

This breakthrough directly inspired DeepSeek-R1, which reproduced and democratized the approach in open-source, proving that this isn't proprietary magic but a reproducible technique accessible to any lab.

o1 marks the moment when LLMs stop merely "completing text" and truly begin to reason.
