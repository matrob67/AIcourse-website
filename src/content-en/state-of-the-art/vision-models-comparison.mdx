## The Three Giants of AI Vision

In 2025-2026, three companies dominate the vision benchmarks: OpenAI, Google, and Anthropic. Each has taken a different architectural approach, and each excels in specific areas. Understanding their respective strengths and weaknesses means being able to choose the right tool for the right problem.

<Callout type="info" title="Two Philosophies: Native vs. Added Multimodal">
**Native multimodal** (Gemini, GPT-5.4): the model is trained from the start on text, images, and audio simultaneously. The different modalities share the same internal representations. **Added multimodal** (early GPT-4V, early Claude): vision is "grafted" onto an existing text model via a separate visual encoder. The native approach tends to produce more fluid cross-modal understanding, while the added approach can be more modular. In 2026, all leading models are natively multimodal.
</Callout>

## GPT-5.4 (OpenAI)

### The Evolution

- **GPT-4V** (November 2023): first version with vision, added on top of the existing text-based GPT-4
- **GPT-4o** (May 2024): the "o" stands for "omni" --- natively multimodal (text, image, audio in a single model)
- **GPT-5** (2025): unification of all models (GPT-4o, o3, o4-mini, GPT-4.1, GPT-4.5) into a single model with integrated reasoning
- **GPT-5.4** (March 2026): current flagship, 1M token context, 33% fewer errors than GPT-5.2. Available as GPT-5.4 Mini and GPT-5.4 Nano for budget-friendly use cases

### Strengths

- **Complex scene understanding**: excels on detail-rich images with many interacting elements --- a crowded street scene, a dense technical diagram
- **Visual reasoning with native reflection**: GPT-5.4 integrates reasoning (inherited from the o1/o3 lineage) natively, which improves logical deductions from visual cues
- **Quality OCR**: reliable reading of fine, noisy, or partially obscured text
- **1M token context**: ability to send many images or long videos in a single request
- **Rich ecosystem**: integrates with DALL-E for generation and function calling for tools

### Limitations

- Object counting remains a recurring weakness across all VLMs
- Proprietary architecture, no access to technical details

## Gemini (Google)

### The Evolution

- **Gemini Pro Vision** (December 2023): Google's first post-PaLM VLM
- **Gemini 1.5 Pro** (April 2024): massive context window (1 million tokens, extendable to 2 million), native video understanding
- **Gemini 2.0** (2025): enhanced agentic capabilities and reinforced multimodal
- **Gemini 3.1 Pro** (February 2026): current model, 1M tokens, dominates 13/16 benchmarks, $2/M input tokens

### Strengths

- **Enormous context**: up to 1 million tokens standard. Concretely, you can send a full one-hour video and ask questions about it
- **Native video**: understands videos frame by frame, detects motion, tracks objects over time. This isn't just isolated image analysis
- **Robust multilingual**: solid performance on documents in various languages, including non-Latin scripts
- **Google integration**: natural connection with Google Workspace, YouTube, Google Search

### Limitations

- Variable performance across benchmarks --- impressive on some, lagging on others
- More aggressive content filtering than competitors on certain types of visual content

## Claude Vision (Anthropic)

### The Evolution

- **Claude 3** (March 2024): introduction of vision in the Claude family
- **Claude 3.5 Sonnet** (June 2024): significant improvement in visual capabilities
- **Claude 4 / Sonnet 4** (2025): even more precise vision with improved reasoning
- **Claude Opus 4.6 / Sonnet 4.6** (February 2026): current models, 1M token context, Claude Sonnet 4.6 reaches 80.8% on SWE-Bench

### Strengths

- **Document analysis**: this is Claude's favorite playground. Complex PDFs, tables with multiple nesting levels, financial charts, invoices --- it dissects them with remarkable precision
- **Structured reasoning**: strong ability for methodical analysis and organizing visual information ("list the anomalies in this chart by order of severity")
- **Computer use**: unique ability to interact with graphical interfaces --- clicking buttons, reading screens, navigating applications. This is an exclusive advantage that no competitor offers at this level
- **Minimal hallucinations**: tendency to say "I'm not sure" rather than making up visual details. Reliable for applications where factual accuracy is paramount

### Limitations

- No image generation (unlike OpenAI with DALL-E)
- No native video support --- only individual frames

## Benchmark Comparison

| Benchmark | GPT-5.4 | Gemini 3.1 Pro | Claude Opus 4.6 |
|---|---|---|---|
| MMMU (multi-discipline) | 74% | 76% | 73% |
| MathVista (math+vision) | 71% | 72% | 69% |
| DocVQA (documents) | 95% | 96% | 97% |
| ChartQA (charts) | 91% | 90% | 94% |
| AI2D (diagrams) | 90% | 91% | 92% |

(2026 update: approximate scores based on the latest published benchmarks.)

A persistent pattern emerges: Gemini 3.1 Pro dominates in general understanding (MMMU), and Claude Opus 4.6 leads on structured document analysis (DocVQA, ChartQA, AI2D). The gaps are narrowing between the three models.

<Callout type="tip" title={"How to Choose Based on Your Use Case"}>
**Documents, tables, charts?** Claude Opus 4.6 remains the best choice --- its dominance on DocVQA and ChartQA is no fluke. **Long videos or massive contexts?** Gemini 3.1 Pro with its 1M tokens and competitive pricing ($2/M input). **General-purpose use, varied images?** GPT-5.4 offers the best overall compromise. **Ultra-tight budget?** GPT-5.4 Nano or Gemini Flash are solid budget options.
</Callout>

## Recommended Use Cases by Model

### GPT-5.4 Is Ideal For
- General-purpose visual assistants that need to understand a bit of everything
- Screenshot and user interface analysis
- Vision + tools integration (function calling) in complex workflows
- Projects where latency is critical (GPT-5.4 Mini/Nano are the fastest)

### Gemini 3.1 Pro Is Ideal For
- Long video analysis (movies, documentaries, meeting recordings)
- Bulky documents (100+ page reports sent in a single request)
- Multilingual applications needing to process documents in many languages
- Any use case where very long context is the differentiating factor

### Claude Opus 4.6 Vision Is Ideal For
- Structured documents: invoices, contracts, accounting reports, forms
- Data extraction from complex tables and charts
- Agents using *computer use* to automate graphical interfaces (next-generation RPA, Robotic Process Automation)
- Applications requiring rigorous analysis where hallucinations are unacceptable

<Callout type="warning" title={"Benchmarks Don't Tell the Whole Story"}>
Scores on MMMU or DocVQA are useful indicators, but they don't always reflect the real-world experience on **your** type of data. A model that scores 2% lower on an academic benchmark may be significantly better on your specific invoices or technical diagrams. Always test on your own data before making a decision. A homemade mini-benchmark of 50 representative examples is worth more than any public leaderboard.
</Callout>

## The Lightning-Fast Open-Source Catch-Up

The gap between proprietary and open-source models is shrinking at an impressive pace:

- **InternVL 2.5**: competitive with proprietary models on several major benchmarks
- **Qwen2-VL**: excellent performance, particularly on document analysis
- **LLaVA-NeXT**: open-source reference with high-resolution support
- **Pixtral**: Mistral's native VLM with an elegant architecture and solid results

What took OpenAI 2 years (from CLIP in 2021 to GPT-4V in 2023) can now be reproduced in a few months by a small team. The pace of open-source innovation is accelerating exponentially.

The emerging trend: proprietary models will maintain a 5-10% lead for the most demanding use cases, but open-source will cover the majority of practical applications. For 90% of vision applications, a well-chosen open-source model will get the job done.
