## The Massive Challenge: Adding the Time Dimension

Generating an image is already a hard problem. Generating a coherent video is an order of magnitude harder. Why?

A 10-second video at 24 frames per second contains 240 frames. If you generate each frame independently (like 240 separate photos), you get visual chaos: characters change faces from one frame to the next, objects appear and disappear, colors oscillate randomly. It's unusable.

**Text-to-video models** must solve two problems simultaneously: generating high-quality individual frames, AND maintaining **temporal coherence** between them. Objects must move fluidly, characters must remain visually consistent, and physics (gravity, inertia, fluids) must make at least some sense.

<Callout type="info" title={"The Complexity Isn't Just Multiplicative"}>
Going from one image to 240 images isn't just "240x the same computation." It's complexity of a different order. The model must understand motion, anticipate physics, maintain object identity over time. It's like the difference between drawing a portrait and making an animated film.
</Callout>

## Sora (OpenAI)

### Architecture: Spatio-Temporal Diffusion Transformer

Sora extends the Diffusion Transformer paradigm to video. The key architectural innovations:

- Videos are compressed into a latent space by a **video VAE (Variational Autoencoder)** (similar to Stable Diffusion's VAE, but in 3D)
- The Diffusion Transformer processes **3D patches**: small cubes that capture both spatial information (image content) and temporal information (motion)
- Attention operates over **the entire space-time** of the clip simultaneously, allowing the model to reason about global coherence
- The model handles videos of **variable** duration and resolution, not just a fixed format

### Impressive Capabilities

- Videos up to 60 seconds in high resolution
- Basic physics understanding: objects fall, water flows, light reflects
- Complex camera movements: zoom, pan, tracking shot, cut changes
- Scenes with multiple coherently interacting characters

### What Doesn't Work Yet

- Physics is sometimes incoherent: objects disappear for no reason, movements defy Newton's laws
- Hands and fine details remain difficult (as in image generation, but amplified by the temporal dimension)
- On long sequences (after 30 seconds), characters can subtly change appearance

## Runway (Gen-3 Alpha)

Runway is one of the commercial pioneers in the field, with rapid progression:

- **Gen-1** (2023): video-to-video --- transforming the style of an existing video
- **Gen-2** (2023): text-to-video and image-to-video --- the first direct generation
- **Gen-3 Alpha** (2024): major quality leap in temporal coherence and resolution

### Runway's Strengths

- **Accessible platform**: intuitive web interface, no need to install anything or know how to code
- **Motion control**: you can guide object trajectories and camera direction
- **Performant image-to-video mode**: you provide a starting image, and the model transforms it into a coherent video. Very useful for maintaining control over the result.
- **Mature creative ecosystem**: tools designed for content creators and video production professionals

## Kling (Kuaishou)

Kling, developed by Chinese giant Kuaishou, surprised the community with its unexpected quality:

- High-quality videos with excellent temporal coherence
- Particularly realistic and natural human movements (walking, gestures, facial expressions)
- Good understanding of object interactions (pouring a liquid, manipulating objects)
- Accessible via the Kling AI app, with a consumer-oriented interface

## Veo (Google DeepMind)

Google's video offering, benefiting from their computational power and data:

- **Veo 1**: first generation capabilities, promising but limited
- **Veo 2** (2024-2025): cinematic quality, 4K resolution, sophisticated camera movements worthy of a cinematographer
- Natural integration with Google's ecosystem (YouTube, Google Cloud)
- Excellent understanding of complex and nuanced prompts, leveraging Google's expertise in language understanding

<Callout type="paper" title="Convergence Toward a Standard Architecture">
All cutting-edge models converge toward the **same base architecture**: a Diffusion Transformer processing spatio-temporal patches in a compressed latent space. What differentiates models from each other is no longer the architecture, but:
- Scale (number of parameters, training duration)
- Training data (volume, quality, diversity)
- Motion and camera control techniques

The architectural blueprint has become standard.
</Callout>

## Major Technical Challenges

### Temporal Coherence

The number 1 challenge in video generation. Everything must remain coherent from one frame to the next:

- Objects must persist and move fluidly (no teleportation)
- Lighting must evolve naturally when the camera moves
- Characters must maintain exactly the same appearance (clothing, face, hairstyle)
- Textures and colors must remain constant over time

This is what distinguishes a convincing video from a succession of slides.

### Physics Simulation

Current models don't learn physics explicitly --- they learn statistical correlations from real videos. Result: they approximately understand that objects fall, water flows, and collisions produce reactions. But this understanding is superficial and fragile: as soon as you leave common cases, the physics becomes absurd.

### Computational Cost

Generating a video is **100 to 1,000 times more expensive** than generating an image of the same resolution. A 1-minute video in high quality can cost several dollars in compute. It's a major barrier to democratization, even though costs are dropping rapidly.

## Complementary Approaches

### Image-to-Video

Rather than starting from nothing (pure text-to-video), you generate a video from a starting image. The advantage: the image provides a solid visual reference, which reduces coherence errors.

- **Stable Video Diffusion**: open-source, based on Stable Diffusion's architecture
- **Luma Dream Machine**: exceptional photographic quality, very popular with creators
- **Runway Gen-3**: among the best image-to-video modes on the market

### Video-to-Video

Transforming an existing video (changing style, setting, season):

- Fewer coherence issues since the motion structure is already given by the source video
- Concrete applications: low-cost special effects, setting changes, artistic stylization

<Callout type="warning" title="Major Societal Implications">
Realistic video generation raises considerable concerns:
- **Video deepfakes**: creating fake videos of real people (politicians, celebrities, loved ones)
- **Disinformation**: fabricating false visual evidence of events that never happened
- **Identity theft**: generating credible videos of someone else

Platforms are working on watermarking (Google's SynthID, C2PA for content traceability) and detecting generated content, but it's a constant race between generation and detection. No definitive solution exists to date.
</Callout>

## Open-Source Models: The Gap Is Shrinking

- **Stable Video Diffusion**: image-to-video model from Stability AI, open-weight
- **CogVideo**: open-source text-to-video from Tsinghua University
- **Open-Sora**: ambitious attempt to reproduce Sora in open-source
- **Mochi**: open-source model from Genmo, promising

The gap between proprietary and open-source remains wider in video than in image, mainly because:
- High-quality video training data is difficult and expensive to obtain
- Computational training costs run into millions of dollars
- Technical challenges (temporal coherence) are inherently more complex

But this gap is visibly shrinking. In 1-2 years, open-source models will likely be competitive with proprietary ones for standard use cases.

## Perspective

Video generation is at the stage where image generation was in 2021-2022: impressive but still far from perfection. Priority improvement areas:

- **Duration**: going from 10 seconds to several minutes of coherent video
- **Resolution**: generalizing 4K and beyond
- **Fine control**: being able to specify the precise movement of each object and each camera cut
- **Realistic physics**: physics simulations that don't betray the generated nature
- **Real-time**: generating or editing a video while watching it

The ultimate goal: video production tools as simple and accessible as presentation tools, enabling anyone to create professional video content from a text description.
