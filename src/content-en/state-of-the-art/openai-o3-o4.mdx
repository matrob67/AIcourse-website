## After o1: Iterating on Reasoning

o1 demonstrated that LLMs could truly reason. The logical next step? Push this capability further and make it more accessible. OpenAI launched o3 and o4-mini to address both of these axes.

A quick note on naming: o2 was skipped to avoid confusion with the British telecom operator O2. But the technical progression is very real and significant at each version.

<Callout type="info" title={"A breakneck pace of innovation"}>
The pace of evolution of reasoning models is remarkable. o1 (September 2024), o3 (early 2025), o4-mini (mid-2025). In less than a year, three generations with tangible gains at each iteration. Since then, OpenAI has unified this entire lineage into GPT-5 (2025) then GPT-5.4 (March 2026): reasoning is now natively integrated, without a separate model.
</Callout>

## o3: Deeper, More Versatile Reasoning

o3 retains the fundamental principle of o1 — the invisible chain of thought — but considerably extends its capabilities.

### Key Improvements

- **Deeper reasoning**: capable of solving problems requiring 100+ chained logical steps, where o1 started to lose the thread
- **Tool use during thinking**: o3 can use tools (web search, code execution) *while* it's thinking, not just before or after. It's like a researcher consulting a reference mid-reasoning
- **Multimodal reasoning**: o3 natively integrates images into its thinking. It can look at a graph, diagram, or photo and reason about it
- **Better calibration**: o3 is better at evaluating its own certainty, which reduces confident but wrong answers

### The Landmark Result: ARC-AGI

ARC-AGI is a benchmark specifically designed to test abstract reasoning — the type of reasoning that humans do naturally but that resisted AI. Finding patterns in grids, extrapolating rules from a few examples.

o3 with a high compute budget achieves scores **close to human level** on this benchmark. This is a result the AI community didn't think was achievable this quickly.

### Adjustable Compute Levels: Choose Your Investment

o3 introduces an important practical innovation: you can **dose the amount of thinking** based on the problem:

- **Low**: quick thinking, few reasoning tokens. For moderate problems
- **Medium**: the standard balance between quality and cost
- **High**: deep thinking, many tokens. For the toughest problems

This is a huge efficiency gain: no more paying top dollar for a simple question.

<Callout type="paper" title={"Adaptivity: a major conceptual advance"}>
Instead of forcing a uniform thinking level on all problems, being able to choose Low/Medium/High allows easy questions to not waste compute, and hard problems to receive enough. It's a step toward truly adaptive intelligence that doses its effort based on difficulty.
</Callout>

## o4-mini: Democratized Reasoning

While o3 pushes the depth of reasoning, o4-mini explores another direction: making reasoning **fast and affordable**.

o4-mini is specifically optimized for STEM problems (Science, Technology, Engineering, Math), with a focus on the performance/cost ratio.

### Positioning in the Lineup

- **Much cheaper** than o3, making reasoning accessible for high-volume applications
- **Significantly reduced latency**: faster responses, suitable for interactive applications
- **Competitive performance** on math and code: not far from o3 for a fraction of the price
- **Same API** as GPT-4o: easy migration from a standard model

### Strengths and Limitations

o4-mini shines on math and code — its home turf. But its general knowledge is less extensive than o3's, and it's not designed for creative or conversational tasks. It's a STEM specialist, not a generalist.

## Comparison: Choosing the Right Model

| Model | Relative Cost | Math | Code | Knowledge | Latency |

| GPT-4o | 1x | Good | Good | Excellent | Fast |

<Callout type="tip" title="Practical decision guide">
**2026 context**: o1, o3, and o4-mini have been retired from ChatGPT and unified into GPT-5. The current model is **GPT-5.4** (March 2026) which natively integrates reasoning with 1M token context. The GPT-5.4 Mini and GPT-5.4 Nano variants offer an excellent quality-to-cost ratio for everyday use.
</Callout>

## The Reasoning Model Ecosystem

OpenAI started the trend, but reasoning has become an intense competitive battleground.

### Competing Proprietary Models
- **Gemini 3.1 Pro (Google)**: 1M tokens, dominates 13/16 benchmarks (2026 update)
- **Claude Opus 4.6 / Sonnet 4.6 (Anthropic)**: visible and auditable reasoning, 1M token context (2026 update)

### Open-Source Alternatives
- **DeepSeek-R1**: free, MIT license, competitive performance with o1. Proof that reasoning isn't reserved for giant labs
- **Qwen-QwQ (Alibaba)**: open-source reasoning, strong on Asian languages
- **Magistral (Mistral)**: transparent reasoning, developed in Europe

Reasoning is no longer a monopoly. It has become a standard capability that every serious model must offer.

## Technical Corner: The "Reasoning as a Service" Paradigm

Reasoning models fundamentally change how AI is billed:

Before: you pay for input tokens + output tokens. That's it.

Now: you also pay for "thinking" — the tokens the model uses to think internally.

$$\text{Cost} = \text{input tokens} + \text{reasoning tokens} + \text{output tokens}$$

The cost is dynamic: an easy problem consumes few reasoning tokens, a doctoral-level problem consumes many. Your bill depends on the difficulty of the questions you ask.

### The Automatic Router Pattern

An increasingly common architecture pattern: a router that analyzes the difficulty of each request and sends it to the appropriate model.

- Simple request &rarr; GPT-5.4 Nano (fast, cheap)
- Complex request &rarr; GPT-5.4 (integrated reasoning)

This optimizes overall cost without sacrificing quality on difficult cases.

<Callout type="warning" title="Reasoning models are not universal">
The o3 vs GPT-4o debate is now obsolete with GPT-5.4 integrating both capabilities. But the principle remains valid: extended reasoning tends to "overthink" simple problems. GPT-5.4 automatically doses reasoning effort based on query complexity.
</Callout>

## The Question That Changed

Before reasoning models, the question was simple: "what's the best model?"

Now, it's become a multi-criteria optimization problem: "which model, for this type of problem, with this budget, within this response time, gives the best result?"

OpenAI's reasoning models have transformed model selection into a true engineering problem.
