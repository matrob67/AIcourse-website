## The Dream: An AI That Sees, Listens, and Speaks Like You

For a long time, AI was a collection of isolated specialists. One model for text (ChatGPT), another for images (DALL-E), a third for audio (Whisper). Each modality had its own model, its own pipeline, its own limitations. You talked to ChatGPT via text, it answered via text. Period.

But real human intelligence doesn't work that way. When you have a conversation, you see your interlocutor's facial expressions, you hear their intonation, you analyze a chart they show you, and you respond by combining all of that instantly.

Unified multimodal models mark the moment when AI starts functioning in this integrated way. **A single model** that understands and generates text, images, audio, and video. No more fragmented pipeline, no more translation between modalities: everything is native.

<Callout type="info" title="The Great Convergence">
The recent history of AI is a story of progressive unification: Transformers unified text processing, the Vision Transformer unified vision, CLIP unified text+image. GPT-4o then GPT-5.4 (OpenAI) and Gemini (Google) take the next step by unifying **all modalities** in a single model.
</Callout>

## GPT-4o: Native Multimodal

The "o" stands for "omni" --- everything, in one. GPT-4o isn't a ChatGPT with added plugins. It's a *natively* multimodal model, designed from the ground up to process all modalities together.

### What Fundamentally Changes

Before GPT-4o, OpenAI's voice pipeline was a three-headed Frankenstein:

Audio &rarr; Whisper &rarr; Text &rarr; GPT-4 &rarr; Text &rarr; TTS &rarr; Audio

Three models in cascade. Result: painful latency, total loss of emotion and intonation, and a choppy conversation that resembled nothing like a natural exchange.

With GPT-4o, all of that disappears:

Audio &rarr; GPT-4o &rarr; Audio

A single model, audio to audio directly. Result: **320 ms latency** --- the natural response time of a human in conversation. It's a qualitative change, not just a quantitative one.

### What You Concretely Gain

- **Preserved intonation**: the model hears the emotion in your voice and takes it into account
- **Natural vocal response**: the model "speaks" with emotion, rhythm, pauses
- **Vocal nuances understood**: sarcasm, hesitation, excitement are detected and influence the response
- **Real-time conversation**: no more artificial delay, you talk as with a real person

## Gemini: Google's Massive Multimodal

Google took an even more ambitious approach: Gemini was trained on **all data types simultaneously** from the start:

- Text (massive corpus, multilingual)
- Images (text-image pairs AND standalone images)
- Audio (transcriptions, music, ambient sounds)
- Video (videos with descriptions AND transcriptions)
- Code (all programming languages)

### Gemini 1.5 Pro: Near-Infinite Context

Gemini's real technical feat? A context window of **1 to 2 million tokens**. To give a sense of scale:

- A **full one-hour video** fits in the context, without cuts or summaries
- A **500-page PDF** can be analyzed in full
- An **entire codebase** can be loaded at once
- You can mix video + text + images + audio in the same context

Concretely, you can load a one-hour lecture and ask "at what point does the speaker talk about machine learning?" and get a precise answer with the timestamp.

<Callout type="paper" title="Native Video Understanding">
Gemini processes video the way a human watches it: sampled frames combined with transcribed audio. No need to manually cut into segments. You can ask precise temporal questions: "at what point does the character change topic?" and get "at 23:47."
</Callout>

### Gemini 2.0: Multimodal Generation

The second generation fills the gaps:

- **Native image generation**: no more need for DALL-E on the side
- **Integrated speech synthesis**: the model speaks directly
- **Agentic capabilities**: Project Astra --- an AI with eyes and ears that interacts with the real world

## Technical Corner: How to Tokenize Images and Audio

The central technical challenge: how do you get an image or sound into a model designed for text? The answer: turn everything into tokens.

### Images

An image is split into **patches** (small squares), and each patch becomes a token. This is the legacy of the Vision Transformer. Let's take a concrete example:

Image $1000 \times 1000$ &rarr; patches $16 \times 16$ &rarr; $\approx 62.5 \times 62.5 = 3906$ tokens approximately.

A single pixel would be too granular (too many tokens), the entire image not enough. A patch is the right compromise. In practice, a standard image consumes between 256 and 2,048 tokens depending on the chosen resolution.

### Audio

Sound is first transformed into a mel spectrogram (a visual representation of frequencies over time, weighted by human perception), then this spectrogram is split into tokens. Order of magnitude: one second of audio produces about 50 tokens.

### Video

Video combines both: sampled frames (typically 1 frame per second) converted into visual tokens, plus audio converted into audio tokens. 10 seconds of video = about 10 images + 10 seconds of audio in tokens.

<Callout type="tip" title="The Multimodal Token Budget">
Each modality consumes tokens from your context window. 1 high-resolution image can cost 1,000 tokens. With 100K tokens of context, you can fit about 50 high-res images OR 100 low-res images OR a short video OR a mix of everything. It's a budget to manage consciously.
</Callout>

## Open-Source Models: Multimodal for Everyone

### Meta Chameleon
An "early fusion" approach: text and images are treated as tokens in the same vocabulary, right from the model's input. This allows naturally interleaving text generation and image generation.

### Emu3 (BAAI)
Images, text, and video are all converted into a common discrete space. The model can understand AND generate multimodal content in both directions.

### LLaVA-OneVision
An extension of LLaVA that handles individual images, image sequences (photo albums), and videos --- all with a single unified model.

## The Challenges: When Modalities Conflict

Training a model on text + images + audio simultaneously is a balancing act. The main problem: improving the model on one modality can **degrade** its performance on another. It's like juggling balls --- when you catch a new one, you risk dropping another.

The solution involves careful balancing of data and losses for each modality. It's as much an art as a science, and every lab has its secret recipes.

## Important Limitations

### Unified Models Aren't the Best at Every Task

For pure image generation, specialists like Flux outperform Gemini. For precision OCR (Optical Character Recognition), LayoutLM remains superior. For high-fidelity speech synthesis, ElevenLabs does better.

<Callout type="warning" title={"Unification: Versatility vs. Excellence"}>
Unified models shine through their **flexibility** and their ability to reason **across modalities**. But they're not the best at every individual task. For an application that needs to do ten things correctly, the generalist wins. For an application that must excel at a single task, the specialist remains king.
</Callout>

## The Future: Toward Universal AI

The direction is clear: a model that sees a scene and deeply understands it, listens and responds naturally, generates visual and textual content, reasons across all modalities together, with the same fluidity as a human.

GPT-4o then GPT-5.4, Gemini 3.1 Pro, and Claude Opus 4.6 have demonstrated that a unified architecture can approach the quality of specialized models while offering incomparable flexibility. In 2026, all leading models are natively multimodal.
