## The Revolution: Creating Images from Words

Say "a tropical forest at dawn, sunbeams filtering through the trees, colorful butterflies" and an AI generates a photorealistic image in seconds. Three years ago, this was science fiction. Today, it's commonplace. **Text-to-image models** have democratized visual creation: you don't need to be a photographer, illustrator, or graphic designer to produce professional-quality images.



<Callout type="info" title={"The Text-to-Image Ecosystem in Two Worlds"}>
On one side, the **proprietary** world: DALL-E 3 (OpenAI), Midjourney, Imagen (Google) --- turnkey services with premium quality. On the other, the **open-source** world: Stable Diffusion, SDXL, Flux --- free models that have catalyzed a massive community of creators. Thousands of LoRAs (fine-tuned adaptations), extensions, and variations are available for free. The open-source ecosystem has become a cultural phenomenon.
</Callout>

## Stable Diffusion: The Founder of the Open-Source Era

### How It Works

Stable Diffusion uses a **Latent Diffusion Model** --- instead of working directly on pixels (which would be extremely expensive), it operates in a compressed space. Four components work together:

1. **VAE Encoder**: compresses the 512x512 image into a 64x64 latent space (48x smaller)
2. **UNet**: the heart of the system, a denoising network that operates in this latent space
3. **Text Encoder**: converts the text prompt into a numerical vector (uses CLIP, a model trained to align text and images)
4. **VAE Decoder**: reconstructs the final image from the latent space


### The Step-by-Step Generation Process

1. Start with random noise in the latent space (64x64x4)
2. The UNet predicts "what noise to remove at this step," guided by the text embedding
3. Remove that noise from the latent image
4. Repeat for T steps (usually 20-50), each step refining the image
5. The VAE decoder reconstructs the final image in pixels (512x512 or higher)

The text embedding intervenes at each step via the cross-attention mechanism: at each denoising iteration, the model "consults" the prompt to know which direction to steer the generation.

### Main Versions

- **SD 1.5** (2022): 860M parameters, 512x512. The most popular thanks to thousands of community fine-tunes. A classic.
- **SD 2.1** (2022): new text encoder (OpenCLIP), results often worse than 1.5 --- a sidestep rather than forward.
- **SDXL** (2023): 6.6B parameters, 1024x1024. A real quality leap. The current standard.
- **SD 3** (2024): DiT (Diffusion Transformer) architecture, triple text encoder (CLIP ViT-L, OpenCLIP ViT-bigG, and T5-XXL). More modern but slower adoption.

## SDXL: The Current Standard in Production

SDXL brings significant improvements over SD 1.5:

- **Native 1024x1024 resolution** (vs 512x512): four times more pixels, much more detail
- **Two-stage architecture**: a base model for global composition, an optional refiner for fine details and textures
- **Dual text encoders**: CLIP ViT-L + OpenCLIP ViT-bigG work together to better understand complex prompts
- **Enriched conditioning**: the model also receives the original image size, cropping, and an aesthetic target, which improves visual coherence

<Callout type="tip" title="Why SDXL Dominates in Production">
SDXL remains the most widely used in real deployment. The reason: an excellent quality/speed/cost compromise, and above all a gigantic community ecosystem. Thousands of LoRAs let you generate in specific styles (manga, pixel art, vintage photo, concept art) without training a full model. Each LoRA is 10-100 MB and applies instantly.
</Callout>

## Flux: The Next Generation

Flux, developed by Black Forest Labs (founded by the original creators of Stable Diffusion), represents a major architectural shift.

### What Fundamentally Changes

- **Diffusion Transformer (DiT)** replaces the UNet: a more powerful and scalable architecture, inherited from the success of Transformers in NLP
- **Flow Matching** replaces classic DDPM: more stable and efficient training, better quality with fewer steps
- **T5-XXL as text encoder**: a much more powerful text encoder than CLIP, capable of understanding long and complex prompts
- **Multimodal attention**: deep fusion of text and image tokens directly in the Transformer, instead of external cross-attention

### The Three Variants

- **Flux.1 [pro]**: maximum quality, accessible only via API
- **Flux.1 [dev]**: open-weight, non-commercial license. For research and experimentation.
- **Flux.1 [schnell]**: generation in just 1-4 steps, near real-time. Apache 2.0 license (fully free). The fastest.

### Why Flux Is a Significant Leap

- **Better instruction following**: when you say "exactly 3 red apples on a blue table," you get exactly that. Stable Diffusion struggled with precise quantities and attributes.
- **Text in images**: Flux generates readable and correct text in images --- a notorious weakness of Stable Diffusion.
- **Realistic human anatomy**: 5-fingered hands, correct proportions, natural poses.
- **Complex compositions**: handles scenes with multiple objects and interacting characters better.

## Key Techniques for Usage

### Classifier-Free Guidance (CFG)

The main technique for controlling whether the image follows the prompt well:

$$\varepsilon_{\text{guided}} = \varepsilon_{\text{uncond}} + w \times (\varepsilon_{\text{cond}} - \varepsilon_{\text{uncond}})$$

The factor w (guidance scale) controls the intensity of guidance. The intuition: the model makes two predictions --- one ignoring the text (epsilon_uncond), one following it (epsilon_cond). We amplify the difference between the two to force the model to better follow the prompt.

Typical values:
- w=7-12 for Stable Diffusion (it needs more guidance)
- w=3.5 for Flux (it naturally follows prompts better, so less guidance needed)

### Samplers: The Denoising Algorithm

The choice of sampler affects speed and quality:

- **DDPM**: 1,000 steps, maximum quality but impractically slow
- **DDIM**: 20-50 steps, deterministic (same seed = same image, perfect for reproducibility)
- **Euler**: 20-30 steps, good general compromise
- **DPM++ 2M Karras**: 20-30 steps, the recommended standard for Stable Diffusion
- **Flow Matching** (Flux): 4-50 steps, very efficient thanks to its optimal formulation

### LoRA: Customizing Without Retraining Everything

LoRA (Low-Rank Adaptation) is a small adaptation module that you "plug" onto a base model to specialize it:

- **Style LoRA**: generate in a specific artistic style (Van Gogh, pixel art, anime, watercolor)
- **Character LoRA**: generate a character consistently across different scenes
- **Concept LoRA**: learn an invented visual concept (a fantasy animal, a type of architecture)

LoRAs are small (10-100 MB), fast to train (a few hours on a single GPU), and combinable with each other. This is what makes the Stable Diffusion ecosystem so rich.

<Callout type="warning" title="Ethical and Legal Questions">
Image generation raises important questions: realistic deepfakes, reproduction of artists' styles without consent or compensation, generation of misleading or manipulative content. Responsible models integrate safety filters and automatic watermarking (like Google's SynthID). Responsible use is a shared responsibility between model creators and users.
</Callout>

## The Ecosystem: Tools for Generating

- **ComfyUI**: node-based interface for creating complex workflows. The most powerful and flexible.
- **Automatic1111**: complete and accessible web interface for Stable Diffusion. The most popular for getting started.
- **Diffusers** (Hugging Face): Python library for programmatic integration into applications.
- **CivitAI**: giant community platform for sharing models, LoRAs, and embeddings.

The ecosystem is mature, active, and community-driven. Hundreds of extensions and models are available for free.

## Perspective

Image generation is evolving toward:
- **Transformer architectures** (DiT) replacing CNNs (convolutional neural networks, like the U-Net), following the same trend as in NLP (natural language processing)
- **More efficient training methods** (Flow Matching rather than DDPM)
- **Real-time generation** (Flux schnell: 1-4 steps, under one second)
- **Pixel-level control** (ControlNet for pose, IP-Adapter for character consistency, inpainting for local editing)

The future is fast, controllable, and accessible models, capable of generating in 1-2 seconds with precise control over every element of the image.
