## The Merger: Uniting Two Rival Cultures

April 2023. Google makes a major strategic decision: merge its two rival AI divisions into a single entity.

**Google Brain** (founded in 2011): applied AI. This is the team that invented the Transformer (2017), created TensorFlow, and formalized scaling laws. The team that turns research into products.

**DeepMind** (founded in 2010 by Demis Hassabis, acquired by Google in 2014 for $500 million): pure fundamental research. AlphaGo (which beat the world champion at Go). AlphaFold (which revolutionized structural biology). AlphaZero. The team that solves problems considered impossible.

The merger, led by **Demis Hassabis**, creates **Google DeepMind**. The goal: centralize forces and compete head-on with OpenAI in the race to AGI (Artificial General Intelligence).

<Callout type="info" title={"The combined legacy"}>
Brain brings products, scaling, TensorFlow, and large-scale deployment experience. DeepMind brings fundamental science, breakthroughs, and a Nobel Prize. Together, it's potentially the most powerful research force in AI. The challenge: getting two very different organizational cultures to collaborate.
</Callout>

## Gemini: The Natively Multimodal Model

### Gemini 1.0 (December 2023): Thinking Multimodal from the Start

Google's first model built **natively multimodal**. Unlike GPT-4 (which was trained on text first, then extended to images), Gemini processes text, images, audio, video, and code in an integrated manner from the start.

Three sizes to cover the full spectrum:
- **Nano**: small, optimized to run directly on phones (Pixel)
- **Pro**: the performance/cost balance
- **Ultra**: maximum power. Surpasses GPT-4 on most benchmarks at launch

### Gemini 1.5 (February 2024): The Context That Changes Everything

The major innovation of Gemini 1.5: the context window.

**1 million tokens**. That's an entire book. A complete codebase. A one-hour video. You can put the source code of a 50,000-line project and ask Gemini to explain it, find bugs, or suggest refactors. You can submit a one-hour conference video and ask for a summary of key points.

Then they pushed further: **2 million tokens** in an experimental version.

The architecture uses **Mixture of Experts (MoE)**: instead of activating all parameters for every request, the model routes to specialized "experts." More efficient for inference.

### Gemini 2.0, 2.5, and 3.1: The Age of Agents

- **Gemini 2.0 Flash**: fast, with **agentic** capabilities. The model can use tools, browse the web, and execute actions. This is the beginning of AI agents at Google.
- **Gemini 2.5 Pro**: the direct challenger to o1/o3. Advanced reasoning with integrated "thinking."
- **Gemini 3.1 Pro** (February 2026): current model, 1M tokens, dominates 13/16 benchmarks. $2/M input tokens, the most competitive price among top-tier models (2026 update).

<Callout type="tip" title={"Google's distribution advantage"}>
Google integrates Gemini **everywhere**: Search, Gmail, Docs, Sheets, Google Workspace, Android, Chrome, Google Cloud. That's **several billion** potential users. No other company can match this distribution. If Gemini doesn't win through raw model power, it can win through omnipresence. The most accessible AI is the one already in your daily tools.
</Callout>

## Gemma: Google's Open-Source Offering

Unlike Gemini (proprietary), **Gemma** is Google's open-source model family:

- **Gemma 2B, 7B**: compact models for research and lightweight applications
- **Gemma 2** (9B and 27B): impressive performance-to-cost ratio. The 27B rivals much larger models.
- Permissive license (commercial use allowed)

**CodeGemma**: specialized for programming, a competitor to Code Llama.

**RecurrentGemma**: an **alternative architecture to the Transformer**, based on linear recurrent models. Linear complexity (not quadratic). This might be a glimpse of the post-Transformer architecture.

**PaliGemma**: a vision-language model. Sees an image and talks about it, describes it, and answers visual questions.

## Research: The World's Center of Gravity

Google DeepMind isn't just a language model lab. It's a **scientific publication machine** that touches dozens of fields.

### Fundamental and Applied Sciences

- **AlphaFold 2/3**: protein structure prediction. Given an amino acid sequence, it predicts the 3D structure with accuracy comparable to experiments. AlphaFold 2 solved a problem that had been open for 50 years in biology. **John Jumper** (project lead) co-received the **2024 Nobel Prize in Chemistry**. This is the first Nobel directly attributable to AI.
- **GNoME**: discovery of **2.2 million new stable materials** through AI simulation. Massive acceleration of materials science.
- **GraphCast/GenCast**: AI weather forecasting. Surpasses traditional physical models (ECMWF) in accuracy and speed. 10-day forecasts in minutes instead of hours.
- **AlphaGeometry/AlphaProof**: mathematical reasoning at the International Mathematical Olympiad (IMO) level. The first AI system to reach silver medal level on Olympiad problems.

### Games and Strategy (The DeepMind Legacy)

- **AlphaGo** (2016): mastery of Go, considered the most complex game for humans
- **AlphaZero** (2017): learns chess, Go, and shogi from scratch, without human data
- **AlphaStar**: Grandmaster-level StarCraft II player

### Algorithmic Efficiency

- **AlphaDev**: discovers **better sorting algorithms** than those written by humans. The discovered algorithms are integrated into LLVM (a foundational compiler used by billions of devices). This is AI that invented optimizations everyone uses without knowing it.

### Safety and Governance

Google DeepMind invests seriously in safety:
- Systematic red teaming of models before deployment
- **Frontier Safety Framework**: risk evaluation framework for the most advanced models
- Interpretability research (understanding model decisions)

<Callout type="paper" title="Google's problem: too much research, not enough products">
Google DeepMind publishes enormously and achieves spectacular breakthroughs. But how do you turn that into products users actually adopt? AlphaFold is revolutionary for biologists, but end users don't see it in their daily lives. Google is a massive, complex organization with slow processes. Turning a lab innovation into a deployed product takes months or years. OpenAI, smaller and more agile, ships products faster.
</Callout>

## Infrastructure: Homegrown TPUs (Tensor Processing Units)

Google makes its own AI processors, giving it a vertical integration advantage:

- **TPU v5e, v5p**: specialized chips for training and inference
- **Trillium (TPU v6)**: the next generation, with significant performance-per-watt gains

The advantage: Google isn't dependent on NVIDIA. It can optimize its hardware for its specific needs. Gemini's training costs are probably lower than those of a comparable model trained on NVIDIA GPUs.

The disadvantage: TPUs are only available through Google Cloud. This doesn't benefit the broader ecosystem.

## The Google Paradox

Google has the **best research** in AI. The **best custom chips**. The **largest distribution** (billions of users). And yet?

ChatGPT dominates public perception. OpenAI dominates the narrative. Claude is winning with developers.

Why? **Product execution.**

The launch of Bard (renamed Gemini) was marred by controversial demos, public errors, and slow adoption. ChatGPT? Simple, accessible, magical from the first try.

Google DeepMind solves the greatest scientific problems in AI. But OpenAI solves the problem of putting AI in everyone's hands.

<Callout type="warning" title="The best lab doesn't make the best product">
Being the best scientific lab in the world doesn't guarantee being the commercial leader. OpenAI has a product obsession. Google has a research obsession. To win the AI race (and potentially the AGI race), you need both. Google has the ingredients, but the recipe still needs perfecting.
</Callout>
