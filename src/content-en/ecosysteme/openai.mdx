## The Rise: From Nonprofit to $150 Billion

**Founded in 2015** by Sam Altman, Elon Musk, Ilya Sutskever, and others. Organized as a nonprofit. Mission: build safe AI for the benefit of humanity.

**The problem**: training state-of-the-art models costs billions of dollars. A nonprofit can't raise those amounts. Philanthropic donors aren't enough to fund a 100,000-GPU cluster.

**2019**: OpenAI creates a "capped-profit" entity. Investors can make money, but with a theoretical cap (100x their initial investment). Microsoft invests $1 billion, then an additional $10 billion. The strategic partnership that changed everything: Azure hosts OpenAI's models, and Microsoft integrates GPT into its products (Office, Bing, GitHub Copilot).

**2024**: estimated valuation of **$150+ billion**. The nonprofit startup became the de facto leader of the AI industry.

<Callout type="info" title={"OpenAI's structural problem"}>
OpenAI was born as a nonprofit with the mission of creating safe AI for humanity. But it created a for-profit structure to raise the necessary funds. This duality creates a permanent tension: can a for-profit structure truly prioritize safety over profits? Critics (including Elon Musk, co-founder) say no. OpenAI argues the two are aligned. The debate remains open.
</Callout>

## The GPT Family: From Scaling to Intelligence

### GPT-3 and 3.5: Proof by Scaling

**GPT-3** (May 2020): 175 billion parameters. The first demonstration that scaling works for LLMs (Large Language Models). Increase the size, performance increases, and capabilities emerge (in-context learning, code generation, translation without specific training).

**GPT-3.5 and ChatGPT** (November 2022): the historic moment. ChatGPT reached **100 million users in 2 months**, the fastest consumer launch in tech history (faster than TikTok, Instagram, or any other app). AI became a mainstream conversation topic. Professors banned ChatGPT. Companies adopted it. The world changed.

### GPT-4 (March 2023): The Qualitative Leap

GPT-4 was a **qualitative leap**, not just a quantitative one:

- **Multimodal**: accepts both text AND images as input. You can show it a photo and ask it to describe it, analyze it, or code the interface it shows.
- **Academic performance**: passes the bar exam in the top 10%. Passes university exams in medicine, law, and engineering.
- **Context**: 32K then 128K tokens. You can put an entire book into the conversation.
- **Reliability**: significantly fewer hallucinations than GPT-3.5, though some remain.

### GPT-4o and 4o mini (2024): Democratization

**GPT-4o** ("omni"): natively multimodal. Text, image, and audio in the same model. Reduced latency for near-real-time interactions.

**GPT-4o mini**: compact and economical version. Designed for large-scale applications where cost per request is critical. Excellent performance for its size and price.

<Callout type="tip" title="The business model invented by OpenAI">
OpenAI invented **freemium for LLMs**: free ChatGPT (with limitations), ChatGPT Plus ($20/month for GPT-4), API billed per token. This model was copied by the entire industry. It became the standard: a free tier for massive adoption, a paid tier for monetization, an API for developers.
</Callout>

## The o Series: The Paradigm Shift

### o1 (September 2024): Reasoning Arrives

o1 marks a fundamental turning point: instead of just increasing model size, OpenAI introduced **compute at inference time**. Spending compute at the moment of thinking, not just during training.

The model produces an **internal chain of thought** before responding. It thinks, verifies, explores alternatives, backtracks when an approach fails. This "thinking time" costs compute, but the results are spectacular:

- **Rivals PhD students** in physics, math, and biology
- **AIME**: 83% on American math competitions (vs. 2-4% for GPT-4)
- **Reasoning**: able to solve complex multi-step problems

### o3 and o4-mini (2025): The Acceleration

**o3**: major reasoning improvement. Records on ARC-AGI (Chollet's abstract reasoning benchmark). Mathematical reasoning near perfection.

**o4-mini**: compact and fast version of the reasoning paradigm.

### GPT-5 and GPT-5.4 (2025-2026): Unification

**GPT-5** (2025) unified all previous models (GPT-4o, o3, o4-mini, GPT-4.1, GPT-4.5) into a single family. Reasoning is now natively integrated, without a separate model.

**GPT-5.4** (March 2026) is the current flagship: 1M token context, 33% fewer errors than GPT-5.2. The GPT-5.4 Mini and GPT-5.4 Nano variants cover cost-effective use cases. The legacy o1, o3, and o4-mini models have been retired from ChatGPT.

**The key paradigm**: scaling reasoning time is as important as scaling parameters. It's a second scaling dimension natively integrated into GPT-5.4.

## Sora: Video Generation

**Announced in February 2024**, Sora generates 60-second videos from text descriptions. The model understands physics (realistic movements, light reflections, interactions between objects). The architecture is a **Diffusion Transformer** operating on spatiotemporal patches.

Sora triggered a race in video generation: Runway Gen-3, Google Veo, Kling (China). And intense debates about the implications for video content creators.

## Other Products in the Ecosystem

**DALL-E 3**: image generation, integrated directly into ChatGPT. Understands complex and nuanced prompts.

**Whisper**: open-source speech recognition. Free. High quality. One of the rare truly open-source OpenAI products.

**Codex / GitHub Copilot**: programming assistance, in partnership with GitHub (Microsoft). The first AI product massively adopted by professional developers.

**GPTs and plugins**: ecosystem of custom extensions. Specialized agents created by users. Capability marketplace.

## The Controversies: The Price of Success

OpenAI faces a growing list of controversies:

**Ilya Sutskever's departure** (2024): co-founder and chief scientist, a major figure in AI, left the company to found SSI (Safe Superintelligence Inc.). His departure was interpreted as an alarm signal about OpenAI's direction.

**The board crisis** (November 2023): Sam Altman was fired by the board, then rehired 5 days later after a near-mutiny by employees. A dramatic episode that revealed the tensions between the safety mission and commercial imperatives.

**Copyright lawsuits**: the New York Times is suing OpenAI for using its articles in training data. Other media outlets and authors are following suit. The outcome of these lawsuits will define the legal framework for AI training.

**Lack of transparency**: growing criticism about the opacity of the training process, safety evaluations, and AGI timelines. The "Open" in OpenAI is increasingly contested.

**Abandoning the nonprofit mission**: critics argue that OpenAI betrayed its original mission by becoming a de facto for-profit company maximizing valuation.

<Callout type="warning" title="Structural tensions">
OpenAI has a mission (safe AI for humanity) and a structure (for-profit maximizing valuation). These two objectives can conflict. When it comes to choosing between releasing a powerful model (good for revenue) and withholding it for safety reasons (good for the mission), which one wins? The 2023 board crisis was precisely this conflict. The resolution favored commercial growth.
</Callout>

## Position: Leader but Not Invulnerable

OpenAI remains the undisputed leader: ChatGPT is the most used AI product in the world, the API is the de facto standard, the best talent works there.

But competition is rising: Anthropic (Claude, excellence in code and safety), Google (Gemini, massive distribution), Meta (LLaMA, open-source dominance), DeepSeek (innovation under constraint, drastically reduced costs).

OpenAI won the first round. The second round will be more contested.
