## The Story: Why Leave OpenAI

In 2021, a group of researchers from OpenAI -- among the most important ones -- left the company. Dario Amodei (VP of Research), Daniela Amodei (VP of Operations), Tom Brown (first author of the GPT-3 paper), and others. The reason? A fundamental disagreement on strategy.

Their conviction: you can't build truly safe AI by just slapping a "safety" layer on after the fact, like sticking an airbag on an already-built car. Safety must be **in the DNA** of the model, in its very design, in its training architecture.

Anthropic was founded on this conviction: develop models as powerful as possible, while placing AI safety research **at the center**, not on the sidelines. This isn't a brake on performance. It's a competitive advantage.

<Callout type="info" title="Safety as a business strategy">
Unlike competitors who see safety as a cost or a constraint, Anthropic turns it into a **commercial positioning**. Large companies (banks, insurance companies, law firms, government agencies) pay a premium for AI they can deploy with confidence. "We build more reliable AI" isn't a slogan. It's a value proposition that justifies higher prices.
</Callout>

## Claude: The Assistant That Acknowledges Its Limits

Claude has evolved through several generations, each marking notable progress:

- **Claude 1 (March 2023)**: 100,000 tokens of context. Anthropic's first real public test. Already noted for its ability to follow complex instructions.
- **Claude 2 (July 2023)**: better at reasoning and code. Extended context window.
- **Claude 3 (March 2024)**: three versions for different needs. **Haiku** (fast and economical), **Sonnet** (the balanced option), **Opus** (maximum power).
- **Claude 3.5 Sonnet (June 2024)**: surpasses GPT-4o on most benchmarks. Becomes the favorite of developers.
- **Claude 4 / Sonnet 4 (2025)**: new agentic capabilities. The model can act on systems, not just answer questions.
- **Claude Opus 4.6 / Sonnet 4.6 (February 2026)**: current models, 1M token context. Claude Sonnet 4.6 reaches 80.8% on SWE-Bench and becomes the default free model on claude.ai.

### What Sets Claude Apart

**Massive context**: 1 million tokens (since Sonnet 4.6). You can put an entire book, a complete legal file, or a medium-sized codebase into a single conversation. The model maintains coherence across the whole thing.

**Nuanced instruction following**: Claude actually reads what you ask. Complex instructions with conditions, exceptions, and priorities? It follows them. This is a strength that professional users notice immediately.

**Excellence in code**: Claude has become the favorite of many developers. The quality of generated code, the ability to understand complex codebases, and precise spec-following make the difference.

**Calibrated honesty**: Claude tends to say "I'm not sure" rather than making up a plausible but false answer. Hallucinations are reduced compared to competitors, though not eliminated.

<Callout type="tip" title="The three-size strategy">
Anthropic offers Haiku, Sonnet, and Opus. Not a single model, but **three**. Haiku for fast and economical tasks (classification, customer support, triage). Sonnet for daily balance (productivity, writing, code). Opus when you need the absolute best (complex analysis, advanced reasoning). Each use case has its optimized tool. Customers don't pay for power they don't need.
</Callout>

## Constitutional AI: Aligning an AI Without an Army of Annotators

The classic problem with RLHF (Reinforcement Learning from Human Feedback): you need **thousands of human annotators** to rate the model's responses. It's slow, expensive, and annotators have contradictory biases (one finds a response too cautious, another too permissive).

Anthropic invented **Constitutional AI** (CAI). The idea is elegant:

1. **Write a constitution**: an explicit set of principles (e.g., "be honest", "refuse harmful content", "respect privacy", "acknowledge uncertainty").

2. **The model critiques itself**: you generate a response, then the model examines it in light of the constitution. "Does this response respect the principle of honesty?"

3. **The model revises itself**: based on its own critique, it improves its response. It's an iterative self-improvement process.

4. **AI trains AI**: instead of human annotators, **another instance of the model** rates whether the revised response follows the constitution. These ratings are used for reinforcement learning.

Result: a process that's **scalable** (no need for thousands of humans), **transparent** (the principles are written and readable), and **consistent** (a single framework of principles, not contradictory annotator opinions).

<Callout type="paper" title={"Constitutional AI and the emergence of alignment"}>
DeepSeek R1 showed that pure RL with just a simple reward can make reasoning emerge. Anthropic showed that RL with a "constitution" can make **alignment** with values emerge. These two discoveries converge: many sophisticated behaviors can emerge without detailed process supervision. You define the objective, and the model finds the path.
</Callout>

## Safety Research: Understanding the Neurons

Anthropic conducts frontier research in **mechanistic interpretability**: understanding how neural networks actually *think*, neuron by neuron, feature by feature.

Key discoveries:

**Superposition**: neurons don't each encode a single concept. They encode **multiple concepts simultaneously**, in a compressed manner. It's as if each instrument in an orchestra played multiple melodies at the same time. It's efficient for the model, but makes interpretation very difficult.

**Sparse autoencoders**: a technique for extracting interpretable "features" from the model's internal representations. Each feature corresponds to an identifiable concept: "the Eiffel Tower", "Python code", "sarcasm", "organic molecules". You can read and understand what the model "knows".

**Scaling monosemanticity**: Anthropic found **millions** of these interpretable features in Claude 3 Sonnet. It's a huge step toward understanding internal workings. If you can identify the features responsible for undesirable behavior, you can potentially modify or remove them.

This research isn't just academic. If you can understand **how** a model makes its decisions, you can detect problems before they manifest in production. That's the promise of interpretability.

## The Products: From Research to Market

- **Claude.ai**: the web interface (free and paid tiers). The direct equivalent of ChatGPT.
- **Claude Code**: a CLI (command-line) agent for programming. The model understands your codebase, navigates files, writes and modifies code.
- **Claude API**: for developers. Integration into applications, pipelines, and systems.
- **Amazon Bedrock**: Claude via AWS (Amazon invested $4 billion in Anthropic). The enterprise distribution par excellence.
- **Computer Use**: Claude can interact with graphical interfaces (click, type, navigate). The foundations for truly autonomous agents.

<Callout type="warning" title={"Anthropic's paradox"}>
Anthropic preaches caution in the face of advanced AI. But it also builds the most powerful models possible. It argues that it's **by being at the frontier** that you can see and mitigate risks. If you don't build the most advanced models, you don't know what safety problems they pose. Critics ask: is this a "responsible race" or a rationalization of the race for profits? The answer depends on whether Anthropic truly maintains its safety investments when commercial pressure intensifies.
</Callout>
