## The Strategy: Democratize to Dominate

Meta could have built its own proprietary API. Copied OpenAI. Sold access to its models through a paid interface.

They chose the exact opposite: **give the models to the world**. For free. With a commercial license.

It's counterintuitive, but the logic is airtight:

1. **Prevent an OpenAI monopoly**: if the best models are proprietary, OpenAI and Microsoft control the ecosystem. Meta doesn't want to depend on a competitor.
2. **Create a Meta ecosystem**: thousands of developers and companies build on LLaMA. Meta's influence in AI grows.
3. **Benefit from free community improvements**: the open-source community optimizes, fine-tunes, and improves the models. Meta reaps the insights.
4. **Reduce cloud providers' bargaining power**: if models are free, AWS/Azure/GCP can't impose excessive prices to host them.

It's Google's Android strategy applied to AI: give away the operating system to control the ecosystem.

<Callout type="info" title="Why the open-source bet works">
Meta is a massive company (2+ billion users on its platforms), but it doesn't have OpenAI's position in consumer AI or the cloud infrastructure of Amazon or Google. Open-source bypasses these problems: instead of selling models, Meta gives them away and benefits from the network effect. It's the "thousand flowers" strategy: let the ecosystem bloom and reap the indirect benefits.
</Callout>

## LLaMA: Four Generations of Revolution

### LLaMA 1 (February 2023): The Spark

- Sizes: 7B, 13B, 33B, 65B parameters
- **LLaMA-13B outperforms GPT-3 (175B)**. How? Compute-optimal training, directly inspired by Chinchilla (more data, smaller model).
- Impact: triggered an explosion of derivatives (Alpaca, Vicuna, WizardLM)
- The weight leak (initially reserved for researchers) accelerated massive adoption

### LLaMA 2 (July 2023): Going Commercial

- 7B, 13B, 70B parameters
- **Permissive commercial license**: commercial use allowed (unless you have more than 700M users)
- **Chat** versions fine-tuned with RLHF (Reinforcement Learning from Human Feedback) for dialogue
- Context: 4,096 tokens

### LLaMA 3 and 3.1 (2024): Maturity

- 8B, 70B, **405B** parameters (the largest open-weight model ever released)
- Context: **128K tokens**. Multiple complete books.
- LLaMA 3.1 405B rivals GPT-4 and Claude 3.5 Sonnet on benchmarks
- 8 languages (not just English)
- Trained on 15 trillion tokens (50x LLaMA 1)

### LLaMA 4 (2025): Efficiency

- **Mixture of Experts** (MoE) architecture: more efficiency, less compute per request
- **Scout** and **Maverick** models
- Scout: 10 million tokens of context (a record for open models)
- Competitive with the best proprietary models on benchmarks

<Callout type="tip" title={"LLaMA by the numbers: the Linux of AI"}>
Hundreds of derived models. Mistral is architecturally inspired by LLaMA. Alpaca showed that fine-tuning LLaMA costs $600. Dozens of companies use LLaMA as a foundation. It has truly become the "Linux of AI": an open base on which an entire ecosystem is built.
</Callout>

## Beyond LLMs: Contributions Across All Domains

Meta AI isn't limited to text. Its contributions span vision, audio, and infrastructure.

### Vision and Multimodal

**SAM** (Segment Anything Model): a model that can segment (cut out) any object in any image, even objects it has never seen. Freely accessible. Massively used in industry (medicine, robotics, mapping). **SAM 2** extends this to video.

**DINOv2**: self-supervised learning of state-of-the-art visual features. No need for annotated data: the model learns to "see" by comparing images to each other.

**ImageBind**: a multimodal embedding space that binds **6 modalities** simultaneously (image, sound, text, motion, video, depth). You can search for a sound that matches an image, or a motion that matches a text.

### Software Infrastructure

**PyTorch**: the most widely used deep learning framework in research and production. Created by Meta. It's the foundation on which virtually all current models are built. An indirect but immense contribution.

**FAISS** (Facebook AI Similarity Search): ultra-fast vector search library. It's the technical foundation of RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation) in practice. When you do semantic search across millions of documents, FAISS is probably behind it.

**Llama Stack**: unified infrastructure for deploying, fine-tuning, and customizing LLaMA models.

### Fundamental Research

**FAIR** (Fundamental AI Research): Meta's research lab, one of the most prolific in the world in terms of publications.

**Yann LeCun**: Meta's Chief AI Scientist. Turing Award laureate (the equivalent of the Nobel Prize in computer science). Central figure of deep learning and vocal advocate of an alternative vision of AI.

## Yann LeCun and the World Models Debate

Yann LeCun says something provocative: autoregressive LLMs (GPT, Claude, Gemini) **won't lead to AGI**. His thesis: predicting the next token isn't understanding the world. It's statistical mimicry, not intelligence.

His alternative vision: **world models**. Models that learn **how the world works**: physics, causality, relationships between objects. Not by generating pixels or tokens, but by predicting **abstract representations** (embeddings).

**V-JEPA** (Video Joint Embedding Predictive Architecture): the concrete project. Instead of predicting a video's pixels (expensive and unnecessary), V-JEPA predicts embeddings (compressed representations). The model learns the structure of the world: "an object thrown in the air falls back down," "a vehicle continues in its direction," without ever explicitly generating pixels.

**JEPA** more broadly is a prediction architecture in latent space, not in data space. It's fundamentally different from the autoregressive approach that dominates today.

<Callout type="paper" title="The fundamental philosophical conflict">
OpenAI, Anthropic, and Google are betting on autoregression: predicting the next token, again and again, until intelligence emerges. LeCun argues that this doesn't capture causal understanding of the world. JEPA learns what's invariant and what changes. It's a fundamentally different bet on the nature of intelligence. Both approaches are actively being developed. Only time will tell which one leads further.
</Callout>

## The Systemic Impact on the AI Ecosystem

Meta has changed the very structure of the AI industry by making it open:

**Democratization**: thousands of companies, researchers, and independent developers use LLaMA. AI is no longer reserved for Big Tech.

**Competition**: open-source models force proprietary models to improve faster AND lower their prices. API prices (GPT-5.4, Claude, Gemini) have dropped dramatically, in part due to pressure from LLaMA and Llama 4.

**Community innovation**: the community has innovated in quantization (running models on consumer GPUs), efficient fine-tuning (QLoRA), RAG, and dozens of other techniques. These innovations benefit everyone.

<Callout type="warning" title="Open-weight doesn't mean open-source">
Let's be precise: LLaMA is **open-weight** (public weights), not **open-source** in the strict sense. The training data remains secret. The training process is only partially documented. License restrictions exist. Open-source purists don't recognize LLaMA as truly open. Projects like **OLMo** (Allen AI) go further by publishing data, code, and intermediate checkpoints.
</Callout>
