## The Landscape in March 2026

Four players dominate the LLM API market: **OpenAI**, **Anthropic**, **Google**, and **Mistral**. Each has its range of models, terminology, API features, and specialties. This comparison brings order to the chaos.

<Callout type="info" title={"This comparison will be outdated quickly"}>
Models change every week. This comparison reflects the state as of **March 22, 2026**. Prices, model names, and features will likely have changed by the time you read this. The goal is to understand the **structure** of each offering and the correspondences between them -- that structure changes much more slowly than version numbers.
</Callout>

## Specialized Models: Each Provider's Niche

Each provider has developed unique specialties:

**OpenAI** -- Deep Research (o3-deep-research, o4-mini-deep-research). Models optimized for multi-step research that can spend dozens of minutes exploring a topic. Unique in the industry.

**Anthropic** -- Computer Use. Claude is the recognized leader for GUI control (clicking, typing, navigating). It's also the inventor of the **MCP** (Model Context Protocol), which has become a de facto standard for connecting LLMs to external tools.

**Google** -- Native multimodality. Gemini natively accepts text, images, audio, and video as input, with the market's largest context window (2M tokens for Gemini 3.1 Pro). **Grounding with Google Search** and **Google Maps** are unique advantages.

**Mistral** -- Open-weight + sovereignty. Models are downloadable and deployable without cloud dependency. **Mistral OCR** is a dedicated product (not just vision). **Voxtral** for audio. **Leanstral** for formal proofs in Lean 4. And **Forge** for sovereign continued pre-training.

Mistral OCR is a dedicated product for text extraction and document understanding. Unlike competitors that do OCR "via their LLM's vision," Mistral has a specialized model.

| | **Mistral OCR 3** | **Google Document AI** | **Azure Document Intelligence** | **AWS Textract** |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Speed** | ~2,000 pages/min | ~1,800 pages/min | ~700 pages/min | Variable |
| **Table accuracy** | 96.6% | ~92% | ~96% | 84.8% |

The price advantage is massive: for 100,000 pages/month, Mistral OCR costs ~$100 vs ~$6,500 for Textract (tables). It's the most competitive OCR product on the market in terms of quality-to-price ratio.

## In Summary: How to Choose

**Choose OpenAI if**: you want the most mature ecosystem, deep research capabilities (o3-deep-research), or Microsoft 365 integration.

**Choose Anthropic if**: safety is critical (finance, healthcare, legal), you need computer use (market leader), or you want the best coding assistant (Claude Code).

**Choose Google if**: you need native multimodality (video, audio), a massive context window (2M tokens), Google Search/Maps grounding, or Google Cloud integration.

**Choose Mistral if**: you want the best value for money, European sovereignty (GDPR, SecNumCloud), the ability to deploy on-premise with open-weight models, a best-in-class document OCR, or massive custom training (Forge). The only European provider on the list, and the only one covering all three major clouds.
