## Why This Glossary?

AI has its own language. And when you read a paper or a technical blog, you often hit a wall of jargon. This glossary is your **Rosetta Stone**: the essential terms, explained simply, with links to resources to go deeper.

## A: The Terms That Start the Journey

- **Activation Function**: non-linear function applied to a neuron's output (ReLU, GELU, SiLU, Sigmoid, Tanh). Without it, a neural network would just be one big linear computation -- you might as well use regression. [PyTorch activations](https://pytorch.org/docs/stable/nn.html#non-linear-activations-weighted-sum-nonlinearity)
- **Adam (Adaptive Moment Estimation)**: optimizer combining momentum and adaptive learning rate. The standard for training Transformers. [Original paper](https://arxiv.org/abs/1412.6980)
- **AdaFactor**: Adam variant with reduced memory footprint, used for very large models. For when you don't have enough VRAM (Video RAM) for Adam.
- **Alignment**: the process of making an AI model conform to human intentions and values. Includes RLHF, DPO, Constitutional AI. The great challenge of modern AI.
- **Attention**: mechanism allowing each token to weigh the importance of other tokens. The heart of the Transformer. [Paper "Attention Is All You Need"](https://arxiv.org/abs/1706.03762)
- **Autoencoder**: network that compresses (encodes) then reconstructs (decodes) its inputs. Used for dimensionality reduction and learning representations.
- **Autoregressive**: model that generates sequences token by token, each token conditioned on the previous ones. It's like writing a sentence word by word without being able to go back. GPT, LLaMA, Mistral = all autoregressive.
- **AGI (Artificial General Intelligence)**: artificial intelligence capable of performing any intellectual task a human can. The holy grail. Nobody really knows when (or if) we'll get there.

## B

- **Backpropagation**: algorithm for computing gradients by propagating error backward. The foundation of all neural network training. Without backprop, no deep learning. [Visual explanation by 3Blue1Brown](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Ilg3gGewQ5U)
- **Batch Size**: number of examples processed simultaneously during a training step. Bigger means more stable but requires more GPU memory.
- **BERT** (Bidirectional Encoder Representations from Transformers): bidirectional encoder model pre-trained with Masked Language Modeling (Google, 2018). The model that democratized transfer learning in NLP. [Paper](https://arxiv.org/abs/1810.04805)
- **Bias (parameter)**: additive term in a linear layer. Often removed in modern LLMs for simplicity.
- **Bias (fairness)**: systematic tendency of a model to produce unfair or incorrect results for certain groups. A major ethical issue.
- **BPE (Byte Pair Encoding)**: tokenization algorithm that iteratively merges the most frequent token pairs. Used by GPT, LLaMA, and the majority of LLMs. [Explained by Hugging Face](https://huggingface.co/learn/nlp-course/chapter6/5)
- **BFloat16**: 16-bit numerical format with the same range as float32 but less precision. The de facto standard for LLM training.

## C

- **Chain-of-Thought (CoT)**: prompting technique where the model reasons step by step before giving its answer. One of the most powerful prompt engineering hacks. [Original paper](https://arxiv.org/abs/2201.11903)
- **Checkpoint**: saved state of a model during training. Your safety net when a training run crashes at 3 AM.
- **CLIP** (Contrastive Language-Image Pre-training): contrastive learning model aligning images and text in a shared space (OpenAI). The foundation of text-guided image generation. [Paper](https://arxiv.org/abs/2103.00020), [GitHub](https://github.com/openai/CLIP)
- **Compute**: computational power, measured in FLOPS or GPU-hours. Key (and expensive) resource for LLM training.
- **Constitutional AI**: alignment method using written principles to guide model behavior. Invented by Anthropic. [Paper](https://arxiv.org/abs/2212.08073)
- **Contrastive Learning**: learning by comparing similar and dissimilar pairs. The engine behind CLIP, SimCLR, and much of self-supervised learning.
- **Cross-Attention**: attention where queries come from one sequence and keys/values from another. Used in encoder-decoders and multimodal models.
- **Cross-Entropy**: standard loss function for classification and language modeling. The loss you see in 90% of training runs.
- **CUDA** (Compute Unified Device Architecture): NVIDIA's parallel GPU computing platform ([NVIDIA](https://developer.nvidia.com/cuda-toolkit)). The foundation of the entire deep learning ecosystem. No CUDA, no deep learning (well, almost).

<Callout type="tip" title="Most important terms">
The fundamental terms to master for understanding modern LLMs are: **Transformer, Attention, Tokenization, Embedding, Fine-tuning, RLHF, Scaling Laws, and MoE**. These concepts form the core foundation for understanding generative AI.
</Callout>

## D

- **Data Augmentation**: techniques to artificially increase dataset size (rotations, paraphrases, etc.). When you don't have enough data, you invent variations.
- **Decoder**: Transformer component that generates text autoregressively. Modern LLMs are "decoder-only" (GPT, LLaMA, Mistral).
- **Deep Learning**: ML subdomain using multi-layer neural networks. That's the "deep" in deep learning: lots of stacked layers.
- **Diffusion Model**: generative model that learns to progressively denoise data. Stable Diffusion, DALL-E, Flux = all diffusion models. [DDPM paper](https://arxiv.org/abs/2006.11239)
- **Distillation**: knowledge transfer from a large model (teacher) to a small model (student). How to get 80% of the performance at 10% of the size. [Original paper (Hinton)](https://arxiv.org/abs/1503.02531)
- **DPO (Direct Preference Optimization)**: alternative to RLHF that optimizes directly on preferences without a separate reward model. Simpler, often just as effective. [Paper](https://arxiv.org/abs/2305.18290)
- **Dropout**: regularization that randomly deactivates neurons during training. Prevents the network from "memorizing" the data.

## E

- **Embedding**: dense vector representation of a token, word, or concept in a high-dimensional space. The brilliant idea that allows models to "understand" semantics. [PyTorch tutorial](https://pytorch.org/tutorials/beginner/nlp/word_embeddings_tutorial.html)
- **Encoder**: Transformer component that produces contextual representations. BERT is an encoder. Useful for classification, semantic search.
- **Epoch**: one complete pass through the entire training dataset.
- **Equivariance**: property of a model whose output transforms predictably when the input is transformed (rotations, translations).

## F

- **Few-Shot Learning**: learning from very few examples, typically via the prompt. You give the LLM 3 examples and it "understands" the task. [GPT-3 paper](https://arxiv.org/abs/2005.14165)
- **Fine-Tuning**: adapting a pre-trained model to a specific task by continuing training on targeted data. The most common way to specialize an LLM. [Hugging Face guide](https://huggingface.co/docs/transformers/training)
- **Flash Attention**: optimized implementation of attention exploiting GPU memory hierarchy. Tri Dao revolutionized inference and training with this trick. [Paper](https://arxiv.org/abs/2205.14135), [GitHub](https://github.com/Dao-AILab/flash-attention)
- **FLOPS**: Floating Point Operations Per Second, a measure of computational power. It's like horsepower for engines, but for GPUs.
- **Foundation Model**: large pre-trained model serving as a base for multiple tasks (GPT, BERT, CLIP). The key concept of the modern AI era.

## G

- **GAN (Generative Adversarial Network)**: generative model based on competition between a generator and a discriminator. Revolutionary in 2014, largely superseded by diffusion models for image generation. [Original paper (Goodfellow)](https://arxiv.org/abs/1406.2661)
- **Gradient**: vector of partial derivatives indicating the direction of greatest loss increase. You go in the opposite direction to minimize.
- **Gradient Descent**: optimization algorithm that adjusts parameters in the direction opposite to the gradient. The fundamental algorithm of all deep learning.
- **GQA (Grouped Query Attention)**: attention variant where multiple query heads share key/value heads, reducing the KV-cache. Used in LLaMA 2/3, Mistral. [Paper](https://arxiv.org/abs/2305.13245)
- **GNN (Graph Neural Network)**: neural network operating on graph-structured data. For molecules, social networks, etc.

<Callout type="info" title="Common notation">
In deep learning: **N** = number of parameters, **D** = number of tokens/data, **C** = compute (FLOPS), **L** = loss, **d_model** = model dimension, **n_layers** = number of layers, **n_heads** = number of attention heads.
</Callout>

## H

- **Hallucination**: generation of factually incorrect content stated confidently by an LLM. The number one problem with LLMs in production. The model doesn't "know" that it doesn't know.
- **HBM (High Bandwidth Memory)**: high-bandwidth GPU memory used in AI accelerators (H100, A100). The bottleneck of LLM inference.
- **Hidden State**: internal representation vector of a neural network layer.
- **Hyperparameter**: configuration parameter that is not learned (learning rate, batch size, number of layers). What YOU choose, not what the model learns.

## I-K

- **In-Context Learning (ICL)**: ability of LLMs to learn from new examples provided in the prompt without updating weights. One of the most surprising properties of large models. [Analysis paper](https://arxiv.org/abs/2301.00234)
- **Inference**: using a trained model to make predictions (as opposed to training). It's the "production" side of ML.
- **Instruction Tuning**: fine-tuning an LLM on instruction/response pairs to make it more useful and obedient. That's how GPT-3 became ChatGPT. [FLAN paper](https://arxiv.org/abs/2210.11416)
- **KV-Cache**: cache of Key and Value matrices to speed up autoregressive generation by avoiding recomputation. Essential for fast LLM inference, but memory-hungry.
- **Knowledge Distillation**: see Distillation.

## L

- **Layer Normalization**: normalization of activations within each layer. Alternative to batch norm, standard in Transformers. [Paper](https://arxiv.org/abs/1607.06450)
- **Learning Rate**: hyperparameter controlling the step size for weight updates. Too large = training explodes. Too small = it never converges. The art of ML is often finding the right learning rate.
- **LLM (Large Language Model)**: large language model pre-trained on text at scale. GPT-4, Claude, LLaMA, Mistral = all LLMs.
- **LoRA (Low-Rank Adaptation)**: efficient fine-tuning method adding low-rank matrices to frozen weights. Allows fine-tuning a 70B model with a single GPU. [Paper](https://arxiv.org/abs/2106.09685), [PEFT library](https://huggingface.co/docs/peft)
- **Loss Function**: function measuring the gap between prediction and ground truth, minimized during training.

## M

- **Masked Language Modeling (MLM)**: pre-training task where the model predicts masked tokens. It's like a giant fill-in-the-blank exercise. Used by BERT.
- **MoE (Mixture of Experts)**: architecture where a router selects a subset of experts for each token. Allows having a huge model without every token activating all parameters. Mixtral, GPT-4 = MoE. [Switch Transformer paper](https://arxiv.org/abs/2101.03961)
- **Multi-Head Attention (MHA)**: mechanism with multiple attention heads in parallel. Each head can focus on a different aspect of the information.
- **Multimodal**: model capable of processing multiple data types (text, image, audio, video). GPT-4o, Gemini, Claude = multimodal.

## N-O

- **NER (Named Entity Recognition)**: identification of named entities in text (people, places, organizations). A classic NLP task.
- **Next Token Prediction**: training task for autoregressive LLMs: predict the next token. That's it. Incredibly simple as an objective, incredibly powerful in practice.
- **NLP (Natural Language Processing)**: automatic processing of natural language. The AI domain that deals with text.
- **Normalization**: techniques to stabilize training (LayerNorm, [RMSNorm](https://arxiv.org/abs/1910.07467), BatchNorm).
- **Overfitting**: when the model memorizes the training data instead of generalizing. It's like a student who memorizes answers by heart instead of understanding the course material.

## P-Q

- **Parameter**: learned weight of the neural network, adjusted during training. When we say "LLaMA 70B," we're talking about 70 billion parameters.
- **Perplexity**: measure of language model quality (exponential of cross-entropy). Lower is better.
- **Pre-training**: initial training of a model on a large unannotated corpus. The most expensive step (millions of dollars for large models).
- **Prompt**: input text provided to the model to guide its generation.
- **Prompt Engineering**: designing effective prompts to get desired results. An art and a science. [OpenAI guide](https://platform.openai.com/docs/guides/prompt-engineering)
- **Quantization**: reducing the numerical precision of weights (FP32 -> INT8, INT4) to reduce size and speed up inference. It's like compressing an MP3: you lose a bit of quality but save enormously on size. [GPTQ guide](https://huggingface.co/docs/transformers/quantization/gptq)
- **QLoRA**: combination of 4-bit quantization and LoRA for efficient fine-tuning. Fine-tune a 65B model on a single 48GB GPU. [Paper](https://arxiv.org/abs/2305.14314)

## R

- **RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation)**: technique combining information retrieval and generation to ground responses in documents. The solution to the hallucination problem. [Original paper](https://arxiv.org/abs/2005.11401)
- **Reinforcement Learning (RL)**: learning through interaction with an environment and reward maximization.
- **RLHF (RL from Human Feedback)**: aligning LLMs via a reward model trained on human preferences. The technique that made ChatGPT "helpful and harmless." [InstructGPT paper](https://arxiv.org/abs/2203.02155)
- **RMSNorm**: simplified normalization using root mean square. Standard in modern LLMs (LLaMA, Mistral). Faster than LayerNorm.
- **RoPE (Rotary Positional Embedding)**: rotary positional encoding, standard in LLaMA, Mistral, and most recent LLMs. [Paper](https://arxiv.org/abs/2104.09864)

## S

- **Scaling Laws**: mathematical relationships between performance and model size/data/compute. The discovery that guides billions in investment: if you double the compute, you know exactly how much performance will improve. [Kaplan paper](https://arxiv.org/abs/2001.08361), [Chinchilla paper](https://arxiv.org/abs/2203.15556)
- **Self-Attention**: mechanism where the sequence attends to itself (Q, K, V come from the same sequence). The heart of the Transformer decoder.
- **Self-Supervised Learning**: learning without explicit labels, the supervisory signal comes from the data itself. LLM pre-training is self-supervised.
- **SFT (Supervised Fine-Tuning)**: supervised fine-tuning on annotated input/output pairs. The step between pre-training and RLHF.
- **Softmax**: function that converts a vector of scores into a probability distribution. Everywhere in deep learning.
- **SSM (State Space Model)**: sequential model with linear complexity, an alternative to the Transformer. [Mamba](https://arxiv.org/abs/2312.00752) is the best-known SSM. Potentially the future for very long sequences.
- **SwiGLU**: activation function combining Swish and GLU, standard in modern LLMs. More performant than ReLU for large models. [Paper](https://arxiv.org/abs/2002.05202)

## T

- **Temperature**: hyperparameter controlling the randomness of generation. T=0 = deterministic (always the same answer). High T = creative (but potentially incoherent).
- **Tensor**: multidimensional array, the fundamental data structure of deep learning. A scalar is a 0D tensor, a vector is 1D, a matrix is 2D, etc.
- **Tokenization**: splitting text into units (tokens) processed by the model. "Hello" might become ["Hel", "lo"]. [Interactive visualizer](https://platform.openai.com/tokenizer)
- **Top-k / Top-p**: sampling strategies limiting candidate tokens during generation. Top-p (nucleus sampling) is generally preferred.
- **Transfer Learning**: using knowledge learned on one task for another task. The founding concept of the entire foundation model era.
- **Transformer**: neural architecture based on attention, the foundation of all modern LLMs. [Paper "Attention Is All You Need" (Vaswani et al., 2017)](https://arxiv.org/abs/1706.03762). The most cited paper of the last decade.

<Callout type="warning" title="Constantly evolving vocabulary">
AI vocabulary evolves rapidly. New terms appear regularly (VLA, flow matching, test-time compute, reasoning models, etc.). This glossary covers the fundamental and most common terms. For emerging terms, [Papers with Code](https://paperswithcode.com/) is an excellent reference.
</Callout>

## U-V

- **Underfitting**: when the model is too simple to capture the patterns in the data. The opposite of overfitting.
- **VAE (Variational Autoencoder)**: generative autoencoder with a probabilistic latent space. Used in the Stable Diffusion pipeline to compress images. [Original paper](https://arxiv.org/abs/1312.6114)
- **ViT (Vision Transformer)**: application of the Transformer to vision by splitting images into patches. Revolutionary: no need for CNNs for vision. [Paper](https://arxiv.org/abs/2010.11929)
- **VLM (Vision-Language Model)**: multimodal model combining image and text understanding. GPT-4V, Gemini, LLaVA = VLMs.

## W-Z

- **Weight Decay**: regularization penalizing overly large weights (L2 regularization). Prevents the model from becoming "too confident" in certain features.
- **Word2Vec**: word embedding method learned through contextual prediction (Mikolov, 2013). The beginning of the embedding revolution. [Paper](https://arxiv.org/abs/1301.3781)
- **World Model**: internal model predicting how the environment evolves based on actions. A key concept for robotics and autonomous AI agents.
- **Zero-Shot**: ability to perform a task without any prior examples. You ask the LLM to translate without ever showing it a translation, and it does it. The magic of large models.
