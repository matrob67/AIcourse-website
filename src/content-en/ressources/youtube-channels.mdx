## Seeing to Understand

Math becomes intuition with animations. Code becomes clear with live coding. Papers become accessible with expert voices. YouTube has become, without exaggeration, **the free university of AI**.

The real advantage of YouTube is that you can learn at your own pace. Pause, rewind, 2x speed when you've got it down. And the best AI creators are exceptional educators -- often better than university professors.

<Callout type="info" title="Why YouTube?">
The advantage of YouTube for learning AI is the combination of **visualizations**, oral explanations, and code demonstrations. Abstract concepts (attention, backpropagation, diffusion) become much more accessible with animations and dynamic diagrams. It's one thing to read the attention formula, it's another to **see** it in action.
</Callout>

## Fundamentals and Popularization

### 3Blue1Brown

[3Blue1Brown](https://www.youtube.com/@3blue1brown) (Grant Sanderson) offers the **best math visualizations** on YouTube. If you've never watched his videos, stop everything and go watch. Seriously.

- [Neural Networks](https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLZHQObOWTQDNU6R1_67000Dx_ZCJB-3pi) (4-video series): the best introduction to neural networks that exists
- [Essence of Linear Algebra](https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLZHQObOWTQDPD3MizzM2xVFitgF8hE_ab): linear algebra like you've never seen it
- Exceptional visualizations that give a **deep intuition** for concepts, not just a surface-level understanding

**Who it's for:** Everyone. From high schoolers to PhDs. It's universally excellent.

### StatQuest with Josh Starmer

[StatQuest](https://www.youtube.com/@statquest) (Josh Starmer) explains statistics and machine learning concepts with disarming clarity:
- Step-by-step explanations, very accessible. Josh breaks down each concept into digestible pieces.
- Complete coverage: regression, decision trees, neural networks, Transformers
- His "BAM!" format has become iconic in the ML community

**Who it's for:** Beginners and intermediates. Perfect for solidifying fundamentals.

### Andrej Karpathy

[Andrej Karpathy](https://www.youtube.com/@AndrejKarpathy) (former AI director at Tesla, OpenAI co-founder) produces reference educational videos. It's like a Michelin-star chef giving you free cooking lessons.

- [Let's build GPT from scratch](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kCc8FmEb1nY): complete build of a mini-GPT in 2 hours. THE video to watch to understand LLMs.
- [Let's build the GPT Tokenizer](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zduSFxRajkE): tokenization explained in detail
- [Neural Networks: Zero to Hero](https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLAqhIrjkxbuWI23v9cThsA9GvCAUhRvKZ): the complete series, from basics to GPT
- Exceptional teaching quality from a top-tier expert

<Callout type="tip" title="Karpathy's series">
Andrej Karpathy's [Neural Networks: Zero to Hero](https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLAqhIrjkxbuWI23v9cThsA9GvCAUhRvKZ) series is probably **the best free course available** for understanding LLMs in depth. It covers fundamentals (backprop, micrograd) all the way to building a complete GPT. If you can only watch one resource, this is it.
</Callout>

## Intermediate and Advanced Level

### Yannic Kilcher

[Yannic Kilcher](https://www.youtube.com/@YannicKilcher) offers detailed and accessible **paper reviews**:
- In-depth analysis of major AI papers, often on the same day they're released
- Coverage of news and controversies (he's not afraid to be critical)
- Excellent for following the latest advances without reading every paper in full

**Who it's for:** Intermediate and advanced. You need to know the basics of deep learning.

### AI Explained

[AI Explained](https://www.youtube.com/@AIExplained-official) (Philip) offers thoughtful analyses of AI news:
- Focus on the implications and context of advances, not just the technical details
- Balanced and well-documented analyses, far from the hype
- Great for understanding the stakes beyond the code

**Who it's for:** Everyone. Excellent for gaining perspective.

### Umar Jamil

[Umar Jamil](https://www.youtube.com/@uaborobot) offers detailed **from-scratch implementations**:
- Coding major architectures (Transformer, ViT, Stable Diffusion, Mamba, LLaMA)
- Line-by-line code explanations. You understand every tensor, every operation.
- Excellent for understanding implementations in depth

**Who it's for:** Those who really want to understand the code behind the architectures.

### Two Minute Papers

[Two Minute Papers](https://www.youtube.com/@TwoMinutePapers) (Karoly Zsolnai-Feher) summarizes the latest advances in AI and graphics:
- Short format (5-10 minutes). Perfect for staying informed without spending hours.
- Excellent visualizations, contagious enthusiasm
- His catchphrase "What a time to be alive!" has become legendary

**Who it's for:** Everyone. The fast food of AI news monitoring (in the best sense).

## Free University Courses

The world's best universities put their courses online for free. It's an incredible privilege that you should take advantage of.

### Stanford Online

[Stanford Online](https://www.youtube.com/@stanfordonline) offers complete courses available for free:

- **[CS229 Machine Learning](https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLoROMvodv4rMiGQp3WXShtMGgzqpfVfbU)**: Andrew Ng's classic course. The foundation.
- **[CS231n Computer Vision](https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PL3FW7Lu3i5JvHM8ljYj-zLfQRF3EO8sYv)**: computer vision with CNNs. Fei-Fei Li and Andrej Karpathy.
- **[CS224N NLP with Deep Learning](https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLoROMvodv4rMFqRtEuo6SGjY4XbRIVRd4)**: NLP and Transformers. Chris Manning.
- **[CS25 Transformers United](https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLoROMvodv4rNiJRchCzutFw5ItR_Z27CM)**: seminars dedicated to Transformers with top-tier guests.

### MIT OpenCourseWare

- **[6.S191 Introduction to Deep Learning](http://introtodeeplearning.com/)** ([YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLtBw6njQRU-rwp5__7C0oIVt26ZgjG9NI)): very popular introductory course, updated every year
- **6.036 Introduction to Machine Learning**: ML fundamentals

<Callout type="paper" title="Neel Nanda's videos">
For mechanistic interpretability (understanding **how** a model reasons internally), [Neel Nanda](https://www.youtube.com/@neaborea)'s (ex-DeepMind, Anthropic) videos are a reference. His series covers Transformers, training mechanics, and interpretability in depth. A fascinating and rapidly expanding field.
</Callout>

[Science Etonnante](https://www.youtube.com/@ScienceEtonnante) (David Louapre) regularly covers AI topics with rigor and good pedagogy:
- High-level science popularization, probably the best French-language science channel
- Videos on neural networks, GPT, and AI

### Thibault Neveu

[Thibault Neveu](https://www.youtube.com/@ThibaultNeveu) offers technical content in French on machine learning and deep learning, with practical tutorials and code.

## Conferences and Talks

### Lex Fridman Podcast

[Lex Fridman](https://www.youtube.com/@lexfridman) does in-depth interviews with AI leaders:
- 2-4 hour conversations with Ilya Sutskever, Yann LeCun, Sam Altman, Demis Hassabis, Andrej Karpathy
- Deep questions about both the technical and philosophical aspects of AI
- Long format that allows going in depth. Perfect for long commutes.

### NeurIPS / ICML / ICLR (Official Channels)

Recordings of presentations from major AI conferences are available for free:
- [NeurIPS Virtual](https://neurips.cc/virtual/): talks from accepted papers
- Keynotes from leading researchers
- Tutorials and workshops -- often the best introductions to a topic

<Callout type="warning" title="Watch out for quality">
YouTube also contains **a lot** of low-quality AI content: exaggerated hype, incorrect information, or commercial content disguised as education. Some red flags: promises of "becoming an AI expert in 30 days," sensationalist titles, lack of sources. Stick to creators with verifiable technical expertise and a track record of reliable content. The channels listed here are all recognized by the community.
</Callout>
