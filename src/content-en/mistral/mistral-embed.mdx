## Mistral Embed: Turning Text into Math

An **embedding** is the conversion of text into a vector (a list of numbers) that captures the **meaning** of that text. Mistral Embed transforms the sentence "The cat sleeps on the couch" into a vector of **1024 numbers**. The magic: two texts with similar meaning will have similar vectors in the mathematical space, even if they use completely different words.

Why is this useful? Because comparing vectors is fast and easy for a computer. Semantic search, clustering, duplicate detection, classification: all these use cases rely on embeddings.

To use an analogy: imagine that each text is a point on a map. Texts about the same topic end up in the same neighborhood. Mistral Embed creates that map.

<Callout type="info" title="Cosine Similarity: The Reference Metric">
To compare two embeddings, you use cosine similarity (the cosine of the angle between the two vectors).
- 1 = the texts are semantically identical
- 0 = the texts are completely unrelated
- -1 = the texts are semantically opposite

In practice, values typically range between 0.2 and 0.95.
</Callout>

### Concrete Use Cases

**Semantic search**: unlike keyword search (which looks for exact matches), semantic search finds documents that are similar **in meaning**. "How to cancel my contract" will also find documents that talk about "termination procedure" or "end of commitment."

The classic pipeline:
1. **Indexing**: each document in your database -> embedding -> storage in a vector database
2. **Query**: the user's question -> embedding
3. **Search**: find the documents whose vectors are closest (cosine similarity)
4. **Enrichment**: provide these documents as context to an LLM to generate the answer

**RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation)**: this is the previous pipeline, completed by a Mistral LLM at the end of the chain to generate the final answer. RAG combines the precision of vector search with the generation capability of the LLM.

<Callout type="tip" title="Consistency: Mistral Embed + Mistral LLM">
Use Mistral Embed for vectorization and Mistral Large for generation. The semantic representations and LLM capabilities are designed to work together. It's more consistent than mixing different providers.
</Callout>

**Zero-shot classification**: you don't have training data for a classifier? No problem. Compare the embedding of your text with the embeddings of descriptions for each category. The closest category = your classification. For example, embeddings of "customer complaint," "technical question," "quote request," then compare with each incoming email.

**Clustering**: automatically group similar texts without needing pre-existing labels. A clustering algorithm like K-means works directly on the embeddings. Useful for discovering themes in a corpus of thousands of documents.

**Duplicate detection**: identify similar or duplicate texts in your database, even if they're worded differently. Two product descriptions that say the same thing with different words? Their embeddings will be very close.

### API Usage

Usage is simple: send a list of texts, receive a list of 1024-dimensional vectors.

Texts can be short (a sentence) or long (up to 8K tokens). For long documents, the recommendation is to split them into **chunks** (text segments) before generating the embeddings.

### Best Practices

**Chunking**: this is the most critical step for the quality of your search system.
- **Ideal size**: 200 to 500 tokens per chunk
- **Too short**: the chunk loses context and the embedding becomes uninformative
- **Too long**: specific information gets diluted in an overly general embedding
- **Natural boundaries**: split at the text's natural boundaries (paragraphs, sections, headings)
- **Overlap**: a 10 to 20% overlap between chunks prevents cutting an idea in half

**Instruction prefixes**: for certain use cases, prefixing the text with an indicator guides the embedding.
For example: "passage: " before indexed documents, and "query: " before user questions. This helps the model produce embeddings optimized for question-answer matching.

<Callout type="warning" title="Limitations of Embeddings">
Embeddings capture the overall meaning of a text but can miss subtle nuances: negations ("this product is NOT good" vs "this product is good"), complex logic, or indirect implications. For fine-grained comparisons ("are this contract and this law contradictory?"), an LLM as a cross-encoder gives better results, at the cost of higher latency.
</Callout>

The choice depends on your constraints: if European sovereignty matters, Mistral Embed is the natural choice among API solutions. If you want free self-hosted, BGE-M3 is an excellent open-source alternative.

### Integrations with Vector Databases

**Pinecone, Weaviate, Qdrant**: these managed vector databases natively support Mistral Embed's 1024-dimensional vectors.

**PostgreSQL + pgvector**: if you want to keep everything in your existing SQL database, pgvector adds vector support to PostgreSQL. Embeddings and relational data in the same place.

**ChromaDB**: a lightweight and simple vector database, perfect for prototyping and moderate-sized projects.
