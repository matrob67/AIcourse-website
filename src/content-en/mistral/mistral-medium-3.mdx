## Mistral Medium 3: The Sweet Spot That Powers Businesses

Imagine you need to buy a company car. The Ferrari (Mistral Large 3) is magnificent, but it costs a fortune in maintenance and gas. The Honda Civic (Mistral Small 4) gets the job done for simple commutes, but lacks punch on the highway. And then there's the Porsche 911: fast, reliable, and the cost per mile stays reasonable. That's exactly where Mistral Medium 3 is positioned.

Concretely, Medium 3 reaches about 90% of Large 3's performance on most tasks, but costs 3x less and runs 2x faster. For the vast majority of businesses, it's the model you should choose.

Using Large 3 for that is like taking a moving truck to pick up a baguette. Medium 3 handles these tasks perfectly, for a fraction of the price.

### What's Under the Hood

**MoE (Mixture of Experts) architecture.** Medium 3 doesn't use all its parameters for every request. Imagine a medical practice with 10 specialists: when a patient comes in with back pain, only the physical therapist and the osteopath are consulted, not the dermatologist or the ophthalmologist. That's exactly what MoE does: for each token, only the relevant "experts" are activated. Result: a model with the capacity of a giant but the compute cost of a much smaller model.

**Long context.** You can submit a 100-page document and it processes the whole thing. No cutoffs, no "sorry, I lost track halfway through." This is crucial for enterprise use cases like contract analysis or report synthesis.

**Solid multilingual support.** French, German, Spanish, Italian, Portuguese, English: Medium 3 shines particularly on European languages. A concrete advantage over many American models that treat French as a second-class citizen.

**Native function calling.** The model can interact with external tools: query a database, run a calculation, call an API. That's what enables building real AI agents that take action, not just chatbots that talk.

### Concrete Enterprise Use Cases

**Document summarization.** You receive 50 emails a day. Medium 3 reads each one and produces a one-sentence summary. At the scale of a 200-person team, that's hours of reading saved, for a negligible cost.

**Classification and triage.** A new message comes into your support system: "Is this a critical bug?" "Is this a feature request?" "Is this just a documentation question?" Medium 3 sorts this with precision and speed. Really complex cases can then be escalated to Large 3.

**Assisted writing.** Need to draft a professional email? Generate a quarterly report? Medium 3 writes at a very solid quality level: it understands tone, respects conventions, and produces natural text without sounding robotic.

**Customer support chatbots.** A customer asks "How do I reset my password?". Medium 3 responds correctly, with a human tone. And when you're handling thousands of requests per day, the cost difference with Large 3 adds up to tens of thousands of dollars per month.

<Callout type="tip" title="The smart router strategy">
The smartest companies don't choose ONE model: they combine them. Simple questions (80% of volume) go to Small 4 (ultra fast and cheap). Normal questions (15%) go to Medium 3. Complex questions (5%) go to Large 3. Result: total cost drops by 50% with no perceptible quality loss. This is called "model routing."
</Callout>

### The Financial Argument: Medium vs Large

**3 to 5x cheaper to operate.** If you process 100,000 requests per day, Medium 3 costs roughly 20% of Large 3's price. For a large enterprise, we're talking hundreds of thousands of dollars in annual savings.

**Less demanding hardware.** In on-premise deployment, Medium 3 requires less powerful GPUs. Less hardware means less electricity, less cooling, a significantly lower total cost of ownership (TCO).

**The decisive test:** take 100 requests representative of your real use cases. Run them on Medium 3 and on Large 3. Compare the results. In 80% of cases, Medium delivers 90% of Large's quality at a third of the price. The remaining 20%? Those are the requests that truly justify Large.

<Callout type="warning" title="Never choose based on benchmarks alone">
Public benchmarks on the internet measure generic competencies. Your use case is specific. An hour of testing on your real data can save you tens of thousands of dollars per year. It's the best investment you'll make.
</Callout>

### Best Practices for Getting the Most Out of Medium 3

**Be specific in your prompts.** Medium 3 responds better to clear instructions. Instead of "summarize this text," write "Summarize this text in 3 sentences maximum, in English, keeping the key figures." Prompt specificity has a direct impact on result quality.

**Provide examples (few-shot).** If you expect a particular format, show it in the prompt. A single well-chosen example can transform the quality of Medium 3's responses, because the model instantly understands the expected pattern.

**Consider fine-tuning for specialized domains.** Working in legal, medical, or finance? Training Medium 3 on your own data will often make it better than Large 3 on your specific domain, while remaining cheaper. It's the best of both worlds.

**The philosophy behind Medium 3:** Mistral aims to maximize value per dollar spent. Medium 3 embodies this vision: 90% of frontier model performance, for 30% of the price. For most businesses, that's all you need.
