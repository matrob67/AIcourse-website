### Recommendations by Concrete Use Case

**Customer support chatbot**
Recommended: **Small 4** (simple, repetitive questions) or **Medium 3** (if questions are technical).
Why? A support chat essentially handles questions that have been asked a thousand times before: "How do I reset my password?", "Where's my package?". Small 4 handles that perfectly and costs 10x less than Large. For technical questions that require reasoning, Medium 3 takes over.

**Document reading and summarization**
Recommended: **Large 3** (long documents with images and tables) or **NeMo 12B** (text only, tight budget).
Why? Large 3 processes up to 256K tokens in a single request, equivalent to an entire book, with image and chart understanding included. If your document is purely text and budget is constrained, NeMo 12B offers 128K tokens of context for a fraction of the price.

**Code writing and autocompletion**
Recommended: **Codestral** (fast autocompletion in the IDE) or **Devstral 2** (refactoring, debugging, complex tasks).
Why? Codestral is optimized for the IDE workflow: you type 3 characters, it completes the line or the entire block. Ultra fast, ultra smooth. Devstral 2 is designed for heavier development tasks: analyzing a bug, refactoring a module, explaining legacy code.

**Mathematical reasoning and complex problems**
Recommended: **Magistral v1.2**.
Why? It's Mistral's "reasoning" model: it thinks step by step before answering, like a human who works through a problem on paper before solving it. For math, logic, and multi-step problems, it's the best choice.

**AI on embedded devices (phone, IoT)**
Recommended: **Ministral 3B** (ultra-lightweight) or **Ministral 8B** (more capable).
Why? These are the only models in the lineup compact enough to run directly on a smartphone or embedded device. Ministral 3B even fits on a Raspberry Pi with aggressive quantization.

**Multi-model architecture for high volumes**
Recommended: Small 4 (80% of simple requests) + Medium 3 (15% of intermediate requests) + Large 3 (5% of complex requests).
Why? It's the most cost-effective strategy. A smart router sorts requests by complexity and sends them to the appropriate model. Savings are massive (often 50%+) with no perceptible quality loss, because the majority of requests are simple.

**Multilingual content (10+ languages)**
Recommended: **NeMo 12B** (good quality-to-price ratio) or **Large 3** (maximum quality).
Why? Both models excel on European languages thanks to the Tekken tokenizer and balanced multilingual training data. A concrete advantage over many English-centric models.

### Deployment Considerations

#### Self-Hosted vs API

| Model | Self-hosted viable? | Minimum GPU (quantized) | API recommended? |
|-------|---------------------|------------------------|-------------------|
| Ministral 3B/8B | Yes (consumer GPU) | RTX 3060 / Jetson | Not needed |
| Ministral 14B | Yes | RTX 4070 | Not needed |
| Mistral 7B | Yes | RTX 3060 | Optional |
| NeMo 12B | Yes | RTX 4070 | Optional |
| Mixtral 8x7B | Yes (2 GPUs) | 2x RTX 4090 | Recommended |
| Mixtral 8x22B | Difficult | 4x A100 80G | Recommended |
| Large 3 | Very demanding | Multi-GPU cluster | Strongly recommended |

The simple rule: if the model fits on your GPU with 4-bit quantization, self-hosting is viable and often more economical long-term. Beyond that, the API is generally the pragmatic choice, unless you have data sovereignty constraints that require on-premise.

<Callout type="warning" title="Test on YOUR data before choosing">
Public benchmarks measure generic competencies. Your use case is unique. Take 50 requests representative of your real users, test Small, Medium, and Large on them, measure quality AND cost. It's an hour of work that can save tens of thousands of dollars per year. The best model is the one that delivers the best quality for your budget on your data.
</Callout>

### Where Is the Mistral Lineup Heading?

**Generalized MoE.** Future models will use Mixture-of-Experts even in compact sizes. It's smarter than just stacking parameters.

**Vision everywhere.** The ability to understand images will become standard across the entire lineup, not just on Pixtral and Large 3.

**More specialization.** Codestral for code, Magistral for reasoning, Leanstral for formal proofs: the trend is toward expert models in a domain, in addition to generalist models.

**Edge and embedded.** Small models will continue to improve. Mistral's goal: performant AI at every scale, from smartphone to sovereign datacenter.
