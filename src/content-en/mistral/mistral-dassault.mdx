## Absolute Sovereignty: Mistral + Dassault + OUTSCALE

Some organizations simply **don't have the right** to use AWS, Azure, or GCP. Public administrations, defense, healthcare, finance: for these sectors, the US Cloud Act (which allows US authorities to access data from American companies, even when hosted in Europe) makes US clouds unusable for sensitive data.

So how do you do AI when you have absolute sovereignty constraints? The answer comes through a three-way partnership: **Dassault Systemes** provides the sovereign cloud via its subsidiary **OUTSCALE**, qualified **SecNumCloud** by ANSSI (the French National Cybersecurity Agency). Mistral deploys its models there. The result is a 100% French solution, end to end.

### The Regulatory Context: Why This Is Necessary

Several regulatory frameworks make American clouds problematic for certain organizations:

**US Cloud Act**: adopted in 2018, it allows American authorities to demand access to data stored by US companies, even if that data is physically hosted in Ireland, France, or elsewhere. For a European administration, that's an unacceptable legal risk.

**OIV (Vital Importance Operators)**: in France, certain organizations are classified as OIV by ANSSI. This includes energy, water, transportation, healthcare, and telecom. These organizations have a legal obligation to use sovereign infrastructure for their sensitive data.

**HDS (Health Data Hosting)**: all health data must be hosted in France, by a certified HDS provider. US clouds are not eligible.

**European public procurement**: the French government and European institutions increasingly require sovereign solutions in their tenders. US clouds are often disqualified outright.

<Callout type="info" title="SecNumCloud: The Reference Certification">
SecNumCloud is the certification issued by ANSSI. It guarantees that the infrastructure is based in the European Union, operated by a European entity, with top-tier security and regular audits. It's the reference standard for cloud sovereignty in France.
</Callout>



### The Three Partners

**Dassault Systemes**: French software giant (CATIA, SolidWorks, 3DEXPERIENCE), with over 50 billion euros in market capitalization. Present in virtually every industrial sector, from aerospace to automotive to pharmaceuticals.

**OUTSCALE**: Dassault Systemes' cloud subsidiary. SecNumCloud-qualified infrastructure, datacenters in France, IaaS and PaaS services. It's the cloud provider that checks all the sovereignty boxes.

**Mistral AI**: brings the artificial intelligence models, deployed on sovereign OUTSCALE infrastructure. AI expertise without compromising on sovereignty.

### Deployment Architecture

The deployment of Mistral on OUTSCALE is designed to meet the strictest requirements:

**NVIDIA GPUs in France**: the GPUs needed for Mistral model inference are provisioned in OUTSCALE datacenters located in France.

**Complete isolation**: each client can have dedicated GPUs, an isolated network, and encrypted storage. For ultra-sensitive organizations (defense, intelligence), this isolation is non-negotiable.

**Compatible API**: the API for accessing Mistral models on OUTSCALE is identical to La Plateforme's. This means if you've already developed an application with Mistral, migrating it to OUTSCALE comes down to changing the endpoint address. Zero application code changes.

**Private network access**: no need to transit through the public internet. Secure direct access between your network and OUTSCALE infrastructure is possible.

<Callout type="tip" title="Migration from La Plateforme">
If you already use Mistral via La Plateforme, migrating to OUTSCALE for sovereignty reasons is trivial: change the API endpoint URL. That's it. Application code, prompts, business logic don't change.
</Callout>

### SecNumCloud Guarantees in Detail

**Legal sovereignty**: the operator is French, outside the scope of the Cloud Act, with no applicable US jurisdiction.

**Technical security**: encryption of data at rest and in transit, strong authentication, strict environment partitioning, complete access logging.

**ANSSI audits**: regular audits verify ongoing compliance of the infrastructure with SecNumCloud requirements.

**Transparency**: the operator is required to publish its security practices, procedures, and organization.
