## Mixtral 8x22B: When MoE Scales Up

Mixtral 8x7B had proven the concept: an open-source MoE model could rival GPT-3.5. But it was a modestly-sized proof of concept. Mixtral 8x22B is the version that plays in the big leagues: 176 billion total parameters, 39 billion activated per token, and a 64K token context.

The result: an open-weight model that directly competes with GPT-4, Claude, and Gemini on many tasks. And all of it, downloadable for free.

### Scaling Up: From 8x7B to 8x22B

The scale-up isn't just a simple size change. Virtually all architecture parameters were increased.

| Parameter | Mixtral 8x7B | Mixtral 8x22B |
|-----------|-------------|--------------|
| Total parameters | 46.7B | 176B |
| Active parameters | 12.9B | 39B |
| Number of experts | 8 | 8 |
| Active experts | 2 | 2 |
| Model dimension | 4096 | 6144 |
| Layers | 32 | 56 |
| Maximum context | 32K | 64K |
| Vocabulary | 32,000 | 32,768 |

Three major changes stand out: the model dimension goes from 4096 to 6144 (wider "neurons," capable of capturing more complex patterns), the number of layers goes from 32 to 56 (deeper reasoning), and the context window doubles to reach 64K tokens.

<Callout type="info" title={"Why keep 8 experts instead of adding more?"}>
One could have imagined 16x22B or 32x22B. Mistral chose to keep 8 experts because this number offers a good trade-off. Increasing the number of experts complicates routing balance (the "winner-take-all" problem gets worse) and can dilute each expert's specialization. Increasing the size of each individual expert proved more effective: bigger experts = more capable experts, without complicating routing.
</Callout>

### 64K Token Context: A Qualitative Shift

Doubling the context to 64K tokens isn't just a bigger number. It's a qualitative shift that opens use cases impossible with 32K.

**Entire documents in one request.** 64K tokens is about 200 pages of text. A complete analysis report, a legal contract, a scientific article with its appendices: everything fits in a single request, without having to chunk the document or set up RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation). The model sees the document in its entirety, which improves analysis coherence.

**Code with rich context.** For code analysis and generation, 64K tokens allow providing complete files, their dependencies, associated tests, and documentation. The model understands the project, not just an isolated fragment.

**Long, coherent conversations.** Multi-turn dialogues with extensive history remain coherent over a much longer duration. The model doesn't "lose the thread" after a few exchanges.

### Performance: The Best Open-Weight Model of Its Era

At the time of its release, Mixtral 8x22B positioned itself as the best available open-weight model, rivaling proprietary models.

**Reasoning.** The model excels at logic and math tasks. The combination of deeper layers (56 vs 32) and larger experts allows it to capture more complex reasoning chains than its predecessor.

**Multilingual.** Mixtral 8x22B significantly improves performance in European languages: French, German, Spanish, Italian, Portuguese. The model also shows better understanding of cultural and linguistic nuances, not just syntax.

**Code generation.** Code performance rivals the best specialized models of the era on HumanEval and MBPP, making it a versatile model capable of coding as well as reasoning.

<Callout type="tip" title="The ideal niche for 8x22B">
With 39B active parameters, Mixtral 8x22B occupies a unique intermediate niche: significantly more performant than Mixtral 8x7B (12.9B active) but more efficient than a dense model of comparable size. A 176B dense model would activate all its parameters for every token; Mixtral only activates 39B. That's the fundamental advantage of MoE: the performance of a giant, the efficiency of a much smaller model.
</Callout>

### Mixtral 8x22B Instruct: Native Function Calling

The Instruct version of Mixtral 8x22B was aligned to follow instructions with precision. Its major innovation: **native function calling**.

The model can receive tool definitions in JSON Schema format, decide when to call them based on the user request, and formulate calls with the correct parameters. Concretely, you tell the model "you have access to a calculator, a database, and a weather tool," and it decides on its own when and how to use them.

This is a fundamental capability for "agentic" applications: AI that doesn't just answer, but acts in the real world by calling APIs and orchestrating workflows.

### Hardware Requirements: The Price of Gigantism

Mixtral 8x22B's size imposes serious hardware constraints, which is its main limitation for wide deployment.

**In fp16 precision:** the model requires approximately 350 GB of VRAM, meaning a cluster of 4 to 8 A100/H100 80GB GPUs. This is not consumer hardware.

**In 4-bit quantization (GPTQ, AWQ):** requirements drop to about 90-100 GB, making the model deployable on 2 A100 80GB GPUs. More accessible, but still demanding.

**Ultra-quantized GGUF versions (2-4 bits):** allow even deployment on a single high-end GPU, with quality degradation to evaluate for your use case.

**Optimized inference frameworks:** vLLM and TGI support MoE inference with expert parallelism, maximizing throughput in production. This is essential for server deployments.

### Comparison with Alternatives of the Era

| Model | Active params | MMLU | HumanEval | Context |
|-------|-------------|------|-----------|---------|
| Mixtral 8x22B | 39B | ~77% | ~45% | 64K |
| LLaMA 2 70B | 70B | ~69% | ~30% | 4K |
| LLaMA 3 70B | 70B | ~79% | ~48% | 8K |
| GPT-3.5 Turbo | N/A | ~70% | ~48% | 16K |

Two striking points: Mixtral 8x22B achieves LLaMA 3 70B performance (a dense model that activates ALL its 70B parameters) while only activating 39B parameters. And its 64K context is 4 to 16 times larger than the alternatives.

<Callout type="warning" title="Limitations against frontier models">
Mixtral 8x22B, despite its remarkable performance, remains below proprietary frontier models (GPT-4, Claude 3 Opus) on the most demanding tasks: complex multi-step reasoning, extremely nuanced analysis. Its main advantage lies elsewhere: it's open-weight, locally deployable, and you maintain total control over your data and inference.
</Callout>

### The Legacy of 8x22B

Mixtral 8x22B confirmed two fundamental things. First, the MoE architecture works at scale for open-weight models, not just at modest size. Second, it's possible to rival proprietary giants by combining intelligent architecture with open weights.

This model served as a foundation for extensive research on MoE inference optimization and influenced the design of MoE models at other labs. It also directly paved the way for Mistral Large 3, which pushes the MoE concept even further with 675B total parameters and a 16:1 total/active ratio.
