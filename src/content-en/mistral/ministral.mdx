## Ministral: AI That Fits in Your Pocket

Everyone's talking about bigger and bigger models. Ministral takes the opposite path: models small enough to run **locally**, on lightweight hardware, without an internet connection.

The Ministral family (3B, 8B, 14B parameters) is specifically optimized for **edge computing** (computation at the network's periphery): NVIDIA Jetson, consumer RTX GPUs, and even powerful CPUs. This is embedded AI, not cloud AI.

### Why Edge Computing?

The cloud isn't always an option. Four situations make local deployment essential:

**Critical latency**: an autonomous vehicle waiting 500ms for a cloud response is an accident waiting to happen. An industrial robot that depends on the internet to make a decision means a halted production line. AI needs to be local, as close to the action as possible.

**Absolute confidentiality**: some data must never leave the local network. Medical data, trade secrets, classified information: with an embedded model, nothing goes through the internet. Confidentiality is guaranteed by design.

**No connectivity**: the factory in a rural area has no fiber. The ship at sea has no 5G. The mining site in a remote area has no Wi-Fi. Embedded AI works offline, period.

**Economy at scale**: deploying AI via the cloud on 10,000 devices costs millions per year in API subscriptions. With embedded deployment, you buy the hardware once, and operational costs are nearly zero after amortization.

<Callout type="info" title={"The edge computing explosion"}>
Embedded AI is a booming market: industrial robots, autonomous vehicles, connected factories, IoT (Internet of Things) devices, smartphones. Ministral is positioned to meet this growing demand.
</Callout>

### Ministral 3B: The Minimalist

**3 billion parameters** -- this is the ultra-lightweight version.

In INT4 quantization (mathematical compression of weights), the model weighs only 1.5 to 2 GB of RAM. It runs on:
- An NVIDIA Jetson Nano (an edge device costing a few hundred dollars)
- A high-end smartphone
- Even an older laptop

**Use cases**: text classification, simple entity extraction, short response generation, embedded voice assistants.

**Limitations**: no complex reasoning, no long text generation, limited multilingual capabilities. It's a simple task model, not a general-purpose model.

### Ministral 8B: The Sweet Spot

**8 billion parameters** -- this is the perfect balance between capability and lightness.

It runs comfortably on an RTX 4070 (consumer graphics card). Its performance is comparable to small cloud models, but it runs entirely locally.

**Use cases**: embedded chatbot, local document analysis, summarization, translation, writing assistance.

**The developer favorite**: being able to prototype and test a model on your own machine, without cloud dependency, without API keys, without network latency, means a radically faster development cycle.

<Callout type="tip" title="The devs' favorite">
Being able to iterate locally, without an internet connection, without a cloud subscription, with responses in milliseconds: that's why Ministral 8B has become the model of choice for development and rapid prototyping in the AI community.
</Callout>

### Ministral 14B: The Upper Limit of Edge

**14 billion parameters** represent the upper limit of what you can reasonably deploy on high-end edge hardware.

Its capabilities approach those of Mistral Small in cloud mode. An RTX 4090 or a decent server with a capable GPU is enough.

**Use cases**: on-premise deployments for businesses, lightweight inference servers, AI workstations for technical teams.

### Dense Architecture: A Deliberate Choice

Unlike the large Mistral models (which use MoE architecture to maximize efficiency), the Ministrals are **dense**: all parameters participate in every generated token.

Why this choice?

**Inference simplicity**: no expert routing, no complex conditional logic. Edge hardware isn't designed to handle MoE subtleties. Simplicity guarantees reliability.

**Predictability**: RAM and CPU consumption is constant and predictable. No variations depending on the request, no unexpected spikes. This is crucial for embedded systems where resources are fixed.

**Efficiency at small scale**: below 15 billion parameters, dense models are often faster than equivalent MoE models, because the overhead of expert routing isn't offset by the gains.

### Optimized for NVIDIA Hardware

Mistral works closely with NVIDIA to optimize the Ministrals:

**NVIDIA Jetson**: ready-to-use TensorRT optimization profiles are available for Jetson edge devices.

**RTX**: the models fully leverage the Tensor Cores of RTX cards for massive performance gains compared to standard PyTorch inference.

**TensorRT**: compile your models to achieve maximum performance on your specific hardware.

### Distribution Formats

| Format | Usage | Advantage |
|--------|-------|-----------|
| Hugging Face (safetensors) | Dev + fine-tuning | Complete HF ecosystem |
| GGUF | CPU/GPU via llama.cpp | Built-in quantization, portable |
| TensorRT | NVIDIA GPU | Maximum performance |
| ONNX | Multi-platform | Portability across different runtimes |

Choose the format that matches your constraint: portability (ONNX), maximum performance (TensorRT), or simplicity (GGUF).

<Callout type="warning" title="Realistic expectations">
A 3B or 8B parameter model isn't GPT-4. Don't expect the same reasoning or long generation capabilities. But for specific, well-defined edge tasks, the performance is excellent. The key: define your task precisely and test whether the quality is sufficient.
</Callout>

### The Economic Calculation: Edge vs Cloud

Once the hardware is purchased, the per-token cost locally approaches zero. No monthly subscription, no usage-based billing.

For massive deployments (10,000 robots in a factory, a fleet of vehicles, an entire production line), the savings compared to cloud are **colossal**. The initial hardware investment is recouped in a few months, after which each inference is essentially free.
