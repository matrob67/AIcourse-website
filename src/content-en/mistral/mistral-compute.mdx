## Mistral Compute: European AI on French Soil

Mistral built its own **datacenter** in Essonne (Ile-de-France). Not rented from AWS. Not hosted at Azure. Not outsourced to GCP. Their servers, their GPUs, their territory.

Why would an AI startup invest tens of millions in concrete and copper? The answer boils down to one word: **sovereignty**. Data and models **never** transit through the United States. And in the long run, owning your infrastructure costs far less than renting it.

### Why Build Your Own Infrastructure?

Four strategic reasons justify this massive investment:

**Legal sovereignty**: the US Cloud Act allows federal authorities to access data stored by American companies, **even if that data is hosted in Europe**. With Mistral Compute, this question no longer arises. Zero legal ambiguity.

**Long-term economics**: renting GPUs in the cloud costs a fortune. As an example, a cluster of 2,000 H100 GPUs in the cloud runs 4 to 6 million dollars per month. By buying the GPUs (approximately 30,000 dollars each) and building the datacenter, the initial investment is around 60 to 70 million dollars, but after 2 to 3 years of amortization, operational costs are drastically lower.

**Guaranteed availability**: the GPU market is tight. Delivery times can stretch to months. Owning your GPUs means never finding yourself on a waiting list when you need compute.

**Custom optimization**: a datacenter designed specifically for LLM training can be optimized down to the finest details: network topology, cooling, power supply. That's an advantage generic clouds can't offer.

### Location: Why Essonne?

The choice of the Ile-de-France region is no coincidence:

**Proximity to headquarters**: Mistral's HQ is in Paris. Having the datacenter less than an hour away greatly facilitates operations, debugging, and maintenance.

**Nuclear electricity**: France produces about 75% of its electricity from nuclear power. It's a **decarbonized**, **stable**, and **relatively inexpensive** energy source. For a datacenter consuming several megawatts continuously, that's a major competitive advantage in terms of carbon footprint and costs.

**European internet hub**: the Paris region is the main internet connectivity hub in Europe. This guarantees low latency to the rest of the continent.

**Research ecosystem**: the Saclay plateau, the top engineering schools, AI research centers. The talent pool is nearby.



### NVIDIA Partnership: Much More Than Hardware

NVIDIA doesn't just sell GPUs to Mistral. The two companies collaborate closely:

**Priority access**: Mistral gets the latest GPU generations early (Hopper H100/H200, and soon Blackwell B200). In a market where delivery times are measured in months, this is a considerable strategic advantage.

**Co-optimization**: NVIDIA teams help optimize the datacenter configuration: network topology, GPU placement, cooling strategies, training parallelism. It's not just hardware placed in a room; it's infrastructure designed jointly.

**Direct technical support**: access to NVIDIA's engineering teams for debugging and optimizing training workflows.

**DGX/HGX servers**: Mistral likely uses NVIDIA's pre-configured servers, specially designed for training massive models, with 8 GPUs per node and optimal NVLink interconnection.

### Estimated Technical Specifications

**GPUs**: several thousand latest-generation NVIDIA GPUs, enough to train models with 675 billion parameters like Mistral Large 3.

**Network**: InfiniBand NDR at 400 Gb/s between compute nodes. NVLink 4.0 within each node. Fat-tree topology to maximize total bandwidth. The network is critical for training parallelism: if GPUs can't communicate fast enough with each other, they spend their time waiting instead of computing.

**Storage**: high-performance NVMe for fast access, plus high-capacity distributed storage for training datasets (which weigh in at tens of terabytes).

**Cooling**: adapted to the extreme thermal density of modern GPUs. A single H100 GPU dissipates about 700 watts. Multiply by several thousand, and you understand the challenge.

**Redundancy**: redundant power supply, backup generators, automatic failover mechanisms. A model training run lasting weeks can't afford an outage.

### Sovereignty: Almost Complete, But Not Total

Mistral Compute offers **software and data sovereignty**: the code, models, and data never leave the European Union. That's the part that matters most for regulatory compliance.

But let's be honest: the GPUs are designed in the United States (NVIDIA) and manufactured in Taiwan (TSMC). Europe doesn't yet have high-performance AI chip manufacturing capability. This is a residual dependency that no one in the world can completely avoid today, since even the United States depends on TSMC.

### Environmental Impact: Nuclear as an Asset

**Consumption**: thousands of GPUs running 24/7 represent several megawatts of electricity consumption. That's considerable.

**The French advantage**: thanks to the French energy mix (75% nuclear), the carbon footprint per kilowatt-hour is among the lowest in the world. An identical datacenter powered by coal or natural gas would have a carbon footprint 5 to 10 times higher.

**Energy efficiency**: PUE (Power Usage Effectiveness) measures the ratio between the total energy consumed by the datacenter and the energy actually used for computation. A PUE of 1.0 would be perfect (all energy goes to computation). Modern datacenters aim for a PUE of 1.1 to 1.3. Mistral targets the industry's best standards.

### A Strong Signal for Europe

Mistral Compute sends a clear message: it is **possible** to build world-class AI infrastructure in Europe, without relying on Silicon Valley for hosting. For clients in regulated industries (defense, healthcare, finance, public administration), it's a credible and concrete alternative to American clouds.
