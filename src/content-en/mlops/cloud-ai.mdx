## Why the cloud for training models?

An H100 GPU costs about 40,000 dollars. You're not going to buy one for a side project. The cloud changes the game: you rent GPUs by the hour, and you only pay for what you use. 30 dollars per hour sounds expensive? Sure. But only for 2 hours, that's 60 dollars instead of 40,000. It's like renting a Ferrari for a day instead of buying one.

The three cloud giants (AWS, Google, Microsoft) offer platforms that manage the entire ML lifecycle: data preparation, training, evaluation, deployment, monitoring. It's complex, sometimes frustrating, but it works. And most importantly, it saves you from managing GPU servers yourself (which is a nightmare in itself).

<Callout type="info" title="Cloud market share in 2025">
AWS: ~31% of the global cloud. Azure: ~25%. Google Cloud: ~11%. But in AI specifically, the picture is more nuanced. Google has exclusive TPUs (see the dedicated chapter). Azure has an exclusive partnership with OpenAI. AWS has the largest existing user base. Each has its unique strengths.
</Callout>

## AWS SageMaker: the maximum ecosystem

**SageMaker** is Amazon's ML Swiss Army knife, launched in 2017. Since then, it hasn't stopped growing: today it's a sprawling platform with dozens of sub-services.

### What it offers

- **Studio**: a web-based IDE built on Jupyter for writing and running code
- **Training**: distributed training across multiple GPUs and multiple machines
- **Processing**: large-scale data pre/post-processing
- **Endpoints**: real-time inference API deployment with auto-scaling
- **Pipelines**: ML workflow orchestration (native CI/CD)
- **Feature Store**: centralized, versioned feature storage
- **Model Registry**: model lifecycle management (staging, production, archival)
- **Ground Truth**: data annotation via a managed human workforce

### Advantages

- **Massive AWS ecosystem**: natively integrates with S3, Lambda, IAM (Identity and Access Management), CloudWatch, and the thousand other AWS services. If your company is already on AWS, everything connects naturally.
- **Latest GPUs available**: H100, A100, and all the latest NVIDIA chips as soon as they launch
- **HyperPod**: if a GPU crashes during a multi-day training run, the job automatically resumes from the last checkpoint. For massive model training, this prevents losing thousands of dollars.
- **Maturity**: extensive documentation, examples, and a large community

### Limitations

- **Complexity**: you need to learn the AWS ecosystem on top of the ML platform. The learning curve is steep.
- **Hidden costs**: GPU instances, storage, networking, endpoints... the bill can explode if you're not watching. A forgotten inference endpoint left running over the weekend hurts.
- **Lock-in** (vendor dependency): once your pipeline is built on SageMaker, migrating to another cloud requires considerable effort

<Callout type="tip" title="JumpStart: the shortcut">
SageMaker JumpStart is a catalog of pre-trained models deployable in a few clicks: Llama, Mistral, Stable Diffusion, and many more. No need to build everything yourself. It's the fastest entry point if you want to test a model without worrying about infrastructure.
</Callout>

## Google Cloud Vertex AI: native AI expertise

**Vertex AI** is Google's ML platform, born from merging several older services (AI Platform, AutoML). It benefits from 20 years of Google ML research: TensorFlow, TPUs, and the experience of serving billions of requests per day.

### What it offers

- **Workbench**: managed notebooks (Jupyter) with pre-configured GPUs
- **Training**: custom or distributed training, on GPUs and TPUs
- **Prediction**: inference endpoints with intelligent auto-scaling
- **Pipelines**: orchestration based on Kubeflow Pipelines
- **Feature Store**: managed and integrated with BigQuery
- **Model Garden**: catalog of diverse models (Gemini, Claude, Llama, Mistral)
- **Search & Conversation**: managed RAG for building chatbots with your own data

### Advantages

- **TPU**: exclusive to Google, often cheaper than NVIDIA GPUs for large-scale training. This is a real competitive advantage.
- **Native Gemini**: Google's flagship model integrated throughout the platform
- **BigQuery ML**: train models directly in SQL, without writing Python. Ideal for data analysts who want to do ML without switching tools.
- **Kubeflow**: robust, open-source orchestration on Kubernetes
- **Open Model Garden**: instead of forcing you to use only Google models, the catalog also offers Claude (Anthropic), Llama (Meta), and others

### Limitations

- **Less complete global ecosystem**: AWS has more ancillary services (but Google is catching up)
- **Documentation**: sometimes lags behind on the latest features
- **Fewer GPU choices**: historically fewer NVIDIA options than AWS (improving)

<Callout type="paper" title={"Model Garden: Google's open approach"}>
Instead of imposing its own models, Google Vertex AI offers a diverse catalog: Gemini (Google), Claude (Anthropic), Llama (Meta), Mistral, and others. You're not forced to use Google models to benefit from Google infrastructure. It's a more open approach than you'd expect from a cloud giant.
</Callout>

## Azure Machine Learning: the Microsoft world

**Azure ML** integrates into the entire Microsoft ecosystem (Office 365, Teams, Power BI, Active Directory) and benefits from a strategic partnership with OpenAI. If your company lives in the Microsoft universe, Azure ML is the natural choice.

### What it offers

- **Studio**: web interface for managing your experiments, models, and endpoints
- **Compute**: managed GPU instances with auto-scaling
- **Endpoints**: managed and serverless deployment
- **Pipelines**: ML workflow orchestration
- **Designer**: a drag-and-drop interface for building pipelines without code
- **Azure OpenAI Service**: access to OpenAI models (GPT-5.4, DALL-E, Whisper) with enterprise guarantees
- **Prompt Flow**: a dedicated tool for developing and evaluating LLM applications

### Advantages

- **Azure OpenAI**: exclusive access to GPT-5.4 in an enterprise cloud with SLA (Service Level Agreement), regulatory compliance, and data residency. For companies that want GPT-5.4 without sending their data to OpenAI directly, this is the solution.
- **Microsoft ecosystem**: Power BI for visualization, Office for reports, Teams for collaboration, Azure AD for identity management. Everything is integrated.
- **Enterprise-ready**: compliance (SOC2, HIPAA, GDPR), security, audit logs. Azure ML checks all the compliance boxes for large enterprises.
- **Responsible AI Dashboard**: built-in tools for measuring fairness, explainability, and reliability of your models
- **Prompt Flow**: a unique tool for developing, testing, and evaluating LLM applications with built-in metrics

### Limitations

- **Interface**: less intuitive than competitors, sometimes labyrinthine
- **ML innovation**: the latest AI features sometimes arrive with a slight delay compared to GCP
- **Microsoft lock-in**: very attractive if you're already in the ecosystem, less relevant otherwise

## Comparison: which one to choose?

| Criteria | AWS SageMaker | GCP Vertex AI | Azure ML |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Maturity** | Very high | High | High |
| **Available GPUs** | H100, A100, all | H100, A100 + TPU | H100, A100 |
| **Flagship models** | Llama 4, Mistral | Gemini 3.1 Pro, Claude | GPT-5.4 (exclusive) |
| **Orchestration** | SageMaker Pipelines | Kubeflow Pipelines | Azure Pipelines |
| **Enterprise/Compliance** | Very good | Good | Excellent |
| **GPU cost** | $$$ | $$ (TPU advantageous) | $$$ |
| **Lock-in** | High | Moderate | High |

<Callout type="warning" title="Cloud lock-in is a real risk">
Each platform creates progressive dependency. Data formats, SDKs, and pipelines are platform-specific. The more you invest in one cloud, the more expensive it is to switch. To minimize the risk: use portable open-source tools (MLflow, Kubeflow, DVC), standard formats (ONNX for models, Docker for environments), and keep an abstraction layer between your code and cloud services.
</Callout>

## How to choose in practice

**Already using AWS for everything else?** SageMaker is the natural choice. Integration with your existing services justifies the decision.

**In the Microsoft universe (Office, Teams, AD)?** Azure ML + Azure OpenAI. The synergy is real.

**Want the best pure AI infrastructure?** Vertex AI with TPUs and Gemini. Google has a technological advantage in AI.

**Don't know and starting from scratch?** Start with whichever offers you the most free credits. All three offer credit programs to get started.

Many large enterprises use all three clouds in parallel: AWS for general infrastructure, GCP for AI and data, Azure for the Office ecosystem. Multi-cloud is complex to manage, but it avoids putting all your eggs in one basket.

The important thing: don't get lost in the tools. The real work is your data and your models, not the platform hosting them.
